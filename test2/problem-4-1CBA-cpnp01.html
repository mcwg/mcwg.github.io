In this exercise we will be sorting the integrals (A)--(L) into three categories, those whose integrand is a known derivative, those that can be found by substitution, and those that can be found using integration by parts.

  1. \(\displaystyle\int 2x\,dx\)
  2. \(\displaystyle\int x(x^2+1)^2\,dx\)
  3. \(\displaystyle\int e^{3t}\,dt\)
  4. \(\displaystyle\int te^t\,dt\)
  5. \(\displaystyle\int \theta\sin(\theta^2)\,d\theta\)
  6. \(\displaystyle\int \cos y\,dy\)
  7. \(\displaystyle\int (1+z)\sqrt{z^2+2z+3}\,dz\)
  8. \(\displaystyle\int \theta\sin\theta \,d\theta\)
  9. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{y}{1+4y^2}\,dy\)
  10. \(\displaystyle\int y\ln y\,dy\)
  11. \(\displaystyle\int \sqrt{t}\,dt\)
  12. \(\displaystyle\int z^2e^z\,dz\)

Use the integral strategy checkers below classify the integrals. Some fit into more than one category, but choose a grouping that puts four integrals in each.
