Note that:

\begin{equation*} \text{value of } Q \text{ at } t+1 = ae^{k(t+1)}=ae^{kt}\cdot e^k = e^k \times Q, \end{equation*}

so \(k\) is related to the factor of growth, but the relation is not so straight forward as with \(b\text{.}\) Each unit of time corresponds to a change by a factor of \(e^k\text{.}\)
