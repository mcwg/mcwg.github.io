1. We want a function of the form \(R(t)=A\,B^t \text{.}\) We know that when \(t=0 \text{,}\) \(R(t)=200\) and when \(t=6 \text{,}\) \(R(t)=100 \text{.}\) Therefore, \(100=200B^{6}\) and \(B\approx0.8909 \text{.}\) So \(R(t)=200(0.8909)^t \text{.}\)

  2. Setting \(R(t)=120\) we have \(120=200(0.8909)^t\) and so \(\displaystyle t=\frac{\ln0.6}{\ln0.8909}\approx4.422 \text{.}\)

  3. Between \(t=0\) and \(t=2\)

    \begin{equation*} \frac{\Delta R(t)}{\Delta t}=\frac{200(0.8909)^2-200(0.8909)^0}{2}=-20.630 \end{equation*}

    Between \(t=2\) and \(t=4\)

    \begin{equation*} \frac{\Delta R(t)}{\Delta t}=\frac{200(0.8909)^4-200(0.8909)^2}{2}=-16.374 \end{equation*}

    Between \(x=4\) and \(x=6\)

    \begin{equation*} \frac{\Delta R(t)}{\Delta t}=\frac{200(0.8909)^6-200(0.8909)^4}{2}=-12.996 \end{equation*}

    Since rates of change are increasing, the graph of \(R(t)\) is concave up.
