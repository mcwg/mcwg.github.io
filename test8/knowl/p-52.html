Thus, \(Q=ae^{kt}\) is decaying if \(k\lt 0\) (since \(e^k\lt 1\) if \(k\lt 0\)) and growing if \(k\gt 0\) (since \(e^k\gt 1\) in that case). \(k\) is called the continuous growth rate and is usually given in percent form. For example, \(k=4.3\%\) corresponds to \(Q=e^{0.034t}\text{.}\)
