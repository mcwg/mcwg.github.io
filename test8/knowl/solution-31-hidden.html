1. The initial value of \(P\) is \(25\text{.}\) The growth rate is \(7.5 \%\) per time unit.

  2. We see on the graph that \(P = 100\) at approximately \(t = 19\text{.}\) We can use graphing technology to estimate \(t\) as accurately as we like.

  3. We substitute \(P = 100\) and use logs to solve for \(t\text{:}\)

    \begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} P \amp = 25(1.075)^t\\ 100 \amp = 25(1.075)^t\\ 4 \amp = (1.075)^t\\ \log(4) \amp = \log(1.075^t)\\ t \log(1.075) \amp = \log(4)\\ t \amp = {{\log(4)} \over {\log(1.075)}} = 19.169. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
