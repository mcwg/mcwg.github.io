Let \(Q(t)\) be the mass of the substance at time \(t \text{,}\) and \(Q_0\) be the initial mass of the substance. Since the substance is decaying at a continuous rate, we know that \(Q(t)=Q_0e^{kt}\) where \(k=-{0.1}\) (The decay is \({10} \%\)). So \(Q(t)=Q_0e^{-{0.1} t} \text{.}\) We want to know when \(Q(t)=\frac{1}{2}Q_0 \text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} Q_0 e^{-{0.1} t} \amp = \frac{1}{2}Q_0 \\ e^{-{0.1} t} \amp = \frac{1}{2} \\ \ln e^{-{0.1} t} \amp = \ln\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) \\ -{0.1} t \amp = \ln\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) \\ t \amp = \frac{\ln\frac{1}{2}}{-{0.1} } \approx 6.931 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

So the half-life is about \(6.9\) minutes.
