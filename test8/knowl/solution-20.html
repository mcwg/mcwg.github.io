Solution 6.7.1.

  1. For a function of the form \(N(t)=ae^{kt} \text{,}\) the value of \(a\) is the population at time \(t=0\) and \(k\) is the continuous growth rate. So the continuous growth rate is \(0.0134=1.34\% \text{.}\)

  2. In year \(t=0 \text{,}\) the population is \(N(0)=a= 7.29\) million.

  3. We want to find \(t\) such that the population of \(7.29\) million doubles to \(14.58\) million. So, for what value of \(t\) does \(N(t)=7.29e^{0.0134t}=14.58 \text{?}\)

    \begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} 7.29e^{0.0134t} \amp = 14.58 \\ e^{0.0134t} \amp = 2 \\ \ln e^{0.0134t} \amp = \ln2 \\ 0.0134 t\amp = \ln 2 \\ t \amp =\frac{\ln 2}{0.0134} \approx 51.7274 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

    So the population will double in approximately \(51.7\) years.

  4. Since \(N(t)\) is in millions, we want to find \(t\) such that \(N(t)= 0.000001 \text{.}\)

    \begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} 7.29e^{0.0134t} \amp = 0.000001 \\ e^{0.0134t} \amp = \frac{0.000001}{7.29}\approx0.000000137 \\ \ln e^{0.0134t} \amp \approx \ln(0.000000137) \\ 0.0134 t\amp \approx \ln(0.000000137) \\ t \amp \approx \frac{\ln(0.000000137)}{0.0134} \approx -1179 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

    According to this model, the population of Washington State was \(1\) person approximately \(1179\) years before 2016. It is unreasonable to suppose the formula extends so far into the past.
