Item 6.5.b.

If the interest is compounded only once a year, the formula we will use is \(P(t)=P_0b^t\) where \(b\) is the percent value of what the investment had been one year earlier. If it is earning \({2}\%\) interest compounded once a year, it is \(10{2}\%\) of what it had been the previous year, so our formula is \(P(t)=P_{0}({1.02})^{t}.\)

Using this new formula, we will now solve

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} P(t) \amp = {3} P_0 \\ P_0({1.02})^{t} \amp = {3} P_{0} \\ ({1.02})^{t} \amp = {3} \\ \log ({1.02})^t \amp = \log {3} \\ t \log {1.02} \amp = \log {3} \\ t \amp = \frac{\log {3} }{\log {1.02}}\approx {55.478} \hbox{ years.} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

So, compounding once a year, it will take about \(55.5\) years for the investment to triple.
