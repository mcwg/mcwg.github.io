Since the goal is to get \(t\) by itself as much as possible, first divide both sides by \({3}\text{,}\) and then use logs.

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} {3}({1.059})^t \amp= {47}\\ {1.059}^t \amp= \frac{{47}}{{3}}\\ \log{({1.059})^t} \amp= \log(\frac{{47}}{{3}})\\ t\log{1.059} \amp= \log(\frac{{47}}{{3}})\\ t \amp= \frac{\log(\frac{{47}}{{3}})}{\log{1.059}}={47.999} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
