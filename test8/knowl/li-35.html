Item 6.6.b.

We use \(Q = 150e^{kt}\) and the fact that the quantity decays from 150 to 75 in 4 hours. Solving for \(k \text{,}\) we have:

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} 150e^{k(4)} \amp = 75 \\ e^{4k} \amp = 0.5 \\ 4k \amp = \ln(0.5) \\ k \amp = \frac{\ln(0.5)}{4} = -0.173. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

The continuous percent decay rate is \(17.3\%\) per hour.
