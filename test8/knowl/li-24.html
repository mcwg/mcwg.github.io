Item 6.1.a.

To find the annual growth rate, we need to find a formula which describes the population, \(P(t) \text{,}\) in terms of the initial population, \(a \text{,}\) and the annual growth factor, \(b \text{.}\) In this case, we know that \(a=11,\!000\) and \(P(3)={18} ,\!000 \text{.}\) But \(P(3)=ab^3=11,\!000b^3 \text{,}\) so

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} {18000} \amp = 11000b^3 \\ b^3 \amp = \frac{{18000} }{11000} \\ b \amp = \left(\frac{{18} }{11}\right)^{\frac{1}{3}}\approx 1.17840 . \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Since \(b\) is the growth factor, we know that, each year, the population is about \({117.84} \%\) of what it had been the previous year, so it is growing at the rate of \({17.84} \%\) each year.
