We let \(N\) represent the amount of nicotine in the body \(t\) hours after it was ingested, and we let \(N_0\) represent the initial amount of nicotine. The half-life tells us that at \(t=2\) the quantity of nicotine is \(0.5N_0 \text{.}\) We substitute this point to find \(k \text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} N \amp = N_0e^{kt} \\ 0.5N_0 \amp = N_0e^{k(2)} \\ 0.5 \amp = e^{2k} \\ \ln(0.5) \amp = 2k \\ k \amp = \frac{\ln(0.5)}{2} = -0.347. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

The continuous decay rate of nicotine is \(34.7\%\) per hour.
