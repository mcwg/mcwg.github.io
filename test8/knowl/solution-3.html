Solution 3.3.1.

To convert to the form \(Q=ae^{kt}\text{,}\) we first say that the right sides of the two equations equal each other (since each equals \(Q\)), and then we solve for \(a\) and \(k\text{.}\) Thus, we have \(ae^{kt}={{35} }({1.11})^{t}\text{.}\) At \(t=0\text{,}\) we can solve for \(a\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} ae^{k\cdot 0} \amp = {{35} }({1.11})^0 \\ a\cdot 1 \amp = {{35} }\cdot 1 \\ a \amp = {{35} }. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Thus, we have \({{35} }e^{kt}={{35} }({1.11})^{t}\text{,}\) and we solve for \(k\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} {{35} }e^{kt} \amp = {{35} }({1.11})^{t} \\ e^{kt} \amp = ({1.11})^t \\ \left(e^k\right)^t \amp = ({1.11})^t \\ e^k \amp = {1.11}\\ \ln e^k \amp = \ln {1.11} \\ k \amp = \ln {1.11} \approx 0.104. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Therefore, the equation is \(Q={{35} }e^{0.104 t}\text{.}\)
