Item 6.7.c.

We want to find \(t\) such that the population of \(7.29\) million doubles to \(14.58\) million. So, for what value of \(t\) does \(N(t)=7.29e^{0.0134t}=14.58 \text{?}\)

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} 7.29e^{0.0134t} \amp = 14.58 \\ e^{0.0134t} \amp = 2 \\ \ln e^{0.0134t} \amp = \ln2 \\ 0.0134 t\amp = \ln 2 \\ t \amp =\frac{\ln 2}{0.0134} \approx 51.7274 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

So the population will double in approximately \(51.7\) years.
