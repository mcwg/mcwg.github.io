Since the growth factor is \({1.146} \text{,}\) the formula for the companyâ€™s profits, \(\Pi \text{,}\) with an initial annual profit of \(a\) and time \(t\) in years, is

\begin{equation*} \Pi = a({1.146} )^t. \end{equation*}

The annual profit doubles for the first time when \(\Pi=2a \text{.}\) Thus, we solve for \(t\) after putting \(\Pi\) equal to \(2a\) to give us the doubling time:

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} 2a \amp = a({1.146} )^t \\ 2 \amp = ({1.146} )^t \\ \log2 \amp = \log({1.146} )^t \\ \log2 \amp = t\log({1.146} ) \\ t \amp = \frac{\log2}{\log({1.146} )} = 5.086. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

So the doubling time is about \(5\) years.
