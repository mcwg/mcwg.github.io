Solution 7.9.1.

Get all expressions containing \(t\) on one side of the equation and everything else on the other side. So we will divide both sides of the equation by \({11}\) and by \(({1.07})^t\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} {11}({1.15})^t \amp = {23}({1.07})^t \\ \frac{{1.15}^t}{{1.07}^t} \amp = \frac{{23}}{{11}}\\ \left(\frac{{1.15}}{{1.07}}\right)^t \amp = \frac{{23}}{{11}}\\ \log{\left( \frac{{1.15}}{{1.07}} \right)^t} \amp = \log\frac{{23}}{{11}}\\ t\log \left( \frac{{1.15}}{{1.07}} \right)\amp = \log\frac{{23}}{{11}}\\ t\amp = \frac{\log({{23}}/{{11}})}{\log({{1.15}}/{{1.07}})}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
