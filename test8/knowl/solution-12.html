Solution 5.4.1.

The growth factor for Einsteinium-253 should be \(1-0.03406=0.96594 \text{,}\) since it is decaying by \(3.406\%\) per day. Therefore, the decay equation starting with a quantity of \(a\) should be:

\begin{equation*} Q=a(0.96594)^t, \end{equation*}

where \(Q\) is quantity remaining and \(t\) is time in days. The half-life will be the value of \(t\) for which \(Q\) is \(a/2 \text{,}\) or half of the initial quantity \(a \text{.}\) Thus, we solve the equation for \(Q=a/2 \text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \frac a2 \amp = a(0.96594)^t \\ \frac12 \amp = (0.96594)^t \\ \log(1/2) \amp = \log (0.96594)^t \\ \log(1/2) \amp = t\log (0.96594) \\ t \amp = \frac{\log(1/2)}{\log (0.96594)} = 20.002. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

So the half-life is about \(20\) days.
