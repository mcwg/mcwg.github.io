Since the goal is to get \(t\) by itself as much as possible, first divide both sides by \({108}\text{,}\) and then use logs.

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} {108}({0.73})^t \amp= {54}\\ {0.73}^t \amp= \frac{{54}}{{108}}\\ \log{({0.73})^t} \amp= \log(\frac{{54}}{{108}})\\ t\log{0.73} \amp= \log(\frac{{54}}{{108}})\\ t \amp= \frac{\log(\frac{{54}}{{108}})}{\log{0.73}}={2.202} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
