Since the growth factor is \({1.05} =1+{0.05} \text{,}\) the formula for the cityâ€™s population, with an initial population of \(a\) and time \(t\) in years, is

\begin{equation*} P = a({1.05} )^t. \end{equation*}

The population doubles for the first time when \(P=2a \text{.}\) Thus, we solve for \(t\) after setting \(P\) equal to \(2a\) to give us the doubling time:

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} 2a \amp = a({1.05} )^t \\ 2 \amp = ({1.05} )^t \\ \log2 \amp = \log({1.05} )^t \\ \log2 \amp = t\log({1.05} ) \\ t \amp = \frac{\log2}{\log({1.05} )} = {14.207}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

So the doubling time is about \(14.2\) years.
