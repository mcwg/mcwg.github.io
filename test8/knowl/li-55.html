Item 6.7.d.

Since \(N(t)\) is in millions, we want to find \(t\) such that \(N(t)= 0.000001 \text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} 7.29e^{0.0134t} \amp = 0.000001 \\ e^{0.0134t} \amp = \frac{0.000001}{7.29}\approx0.000000137 \\ \ln e^{0.0134t} \amp \approx \ln(0.000000137) \\ 0.0134 t\amp \approx \ln(0.000000137) \\ t \amp \approx \frac{\ln(0.000000137)}{0.0134} \approx -1179 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

According to this model, the population of Washington State was \(1\) person approximately \(1179\) years before 2016. It is unreasonable to suppose the formula extends so far into the past.
