Solution 3.4.1.

To convert to the form \(Q=ae^{kt}\text{,}\) we first say that the right sides of the two equations equal each other (since each equals \(Q\)), and then we solve for \(a\) and \(k\text{.}\) Thus, we have \(ae^{kt}= {10} (0.862)^{{1.4} t}\text{.}\) At \(t=0\text{,}\) we can solve for \(a\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} ae^{k\cdot 0} \amp= {10} (0.862)^0\\ a\cdot 1 \amp= {10} \cdot 1\\ a \amp= {10}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Thus, we have \({10} e^{kt}={10} (0.862)^{{1.4} t}\text{,}\) and we solve for \(k\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} {10} e^{kt} \amp= {10} (0.862)^{{1.4} t}\\ e^{kt} \amp= \left(0.862^{{1.4} }\right)^t\\ \left(e^k\right)^t \amp= (0.812 )^t\\ e^k \amp= 0.812 \\ \ln e^k \amp= \ln 0.812 \\ k \amp= -0.208. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Therefore, the equation is \(Q={10} e^{-0.208 t}\text{.}\)
