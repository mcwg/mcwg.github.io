Solution 3.1.1.

The continuous percent growth rate is the value of \(k\) in the equation \(Q=ae^{kt}\text{,}\) which is \({8}\text{.}\)

To convert to the form \(Q=ab^t\text{,}\) we first say that the right sides of the two equations equal each other (since each equals \(Q\)), and then we solve for \(a\) and \(b\text{.}\) Thus, we have

\(ab^{t}={2} e^{{8} t}\text{.}\) At \(t=0\text{,}\) we can solve for \(a\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} ab^{0} \amp = {2} e^{{8} \cdot 0}\\ a\cdot 1 \amp = {2} \cdot 1\\ a \amp = {2} . \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Thus, we have \({2} b^{t}={2} e^{{8} t}\text{,}\) and we solve for \(b\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} {2} b^t \amp = {2} e^{{8} t}\\ b^{t} \amp = e^{{8} t}\\ b^t \amp = \left(e^{8} \right)^t\\ b \amp = e^{8} \approx2980.960. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Therefore, the equation is \(Q={2} \cdot 2980.960^{t}\text{.}\)
