Section 5 Third set: Finding the doubling time or half life

Only functions of the form \(Q=ab^t\) and \(Q=ae^{kt}\) have the amazing property that \(Q\) always doubles of reduces in half in the same constant intervals.

To find the doubling time in any of the forms, just set \(Q=2a\) and solve for \(t\text{!}\) (remember that \(a\) is the initial amount, so one is finding the time in which the initial amount doubles). The fact that you can solve it independent of the value of \(a\) or \(t\) is the indication that that it is always constant.

If one solves \(Q=2a\) for \(t\) using general parameters, then one gets a general formula for the doubling time in terms of the parameters which is useful if one is going to be using this repeatedly. Otherwise, it is better just to solve \(Q=2a\) each time one needs it instead of relying on memory.

Similarly, to find the half life in any of the forms, just set \(Q=(1/2)a\) and solve for \(t\text{!}\) (with parameters for a general formula, or with numbers for a specific case).

Subsection 5.2 Wrap-Up

  1. As stated above, only functions of the form \(Q=ab^t\) and \(Q=ae^{kt}\) have the amazing property that \(Q\) always doubles in the same constant intervals. Find a general formula for the doubling time in terms of \(b\) or \(k\) by solving the equation \(Q=2a\) for \(t\) with both forms \(Q=ab^t\) and \(Q=ae^{kt}\text{.}\)

  2. As stated above, only functions of the form \(Q=ab^t\) and \(Q=ae^{kt}\) have the amazing property that \(Q\) always reduces in half in the same constant intervals. Find a general formula for the doubling time in terms of \(b\) or \(k\) by solving the equation \(Q=2/a\) for \(t\) with both forms \(Q=ab^t\) and \(Q=ae^{kt}\text{.}\)
