Section 6 Fourth set: Putting this all together - modeling in situations involving exponential change

Remember:

  • Anything that is changing by a constant factor (or percentage) for every constant change of \(t\) can be modeled in the form \(Q=ab^t\) or \(Q=ae^{kt}\text{.}\)
  • The forms \(Q=ab^t\) and \(Q=ae^{kt}\) are completely interchangeable, but each is more useful than the other depending on the context and the interpretation that \(b\) and \(k\) have.

Keep in mind the convention that the term continuous rate of change is used for the \(k\) in the form \(Q=ae^{kt}\text{.}\)
