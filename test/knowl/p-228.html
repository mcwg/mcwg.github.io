A function \(q\) has all of the following properties:

  • It is continuous everywhere.
  • The zeros of its derivative \(q'\) are at \(x = -3\text{,}\) \(x = 1\text{,}\) and \(x = 9\text{.}\)
  • The derivative \(q'\) is undefined at \(x = 4\text{,}\) but defined everywhere else.
  • We have \(q'(-6) = 2\text{,}\) \(q'(0) = 5\text{,}\) \(q'(7) = 1\text{,}\) and \(q'(12) = -4\text{.}\)
