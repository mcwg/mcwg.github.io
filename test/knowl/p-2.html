A critical point of a function \(f\) is a point where the value of the derivative \(f'\) is either zero or undefined. Critical points of a function are often, though not always, local maxima and minima of the function. A local maximum of a function \(f\) is a point \(a\) such that for all \(x\) near \(a\text{,}\) \(f(x) \leq f(a)\text{.}\) A local minimum of a function \(f\) is a point \(a\) such that for all \(x\) near \(a\text{,}\) \(f(x) \geq f(a)\text{.}\)
