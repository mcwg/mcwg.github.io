Function (i). We see that:

\begin{align*} \cos x-x\sin x\amp=\frac d{dx}\left(x\cos x\right) \amp \text{ Product rule }\\ \int\left(\cos x-x\sin x\right)\,dx\amp=\int\frac d{dx}\left(x\cos x\right)\,dx\\ \amp=x\cos x+C \amp \text{ Fundamental Theorem}\\ \int\cos x\,dx-\int x\sin x\,dx\amp=x\cos x+C \\ \int-x\sin x\,dx\amp=x\cos x-\int\cos x\,dx \\ \amp=x\cos x-\sin x+C, \end{align*}

so knowing \(\displaystyle\frac d{dx}\left(x\cos x\right)\) helps us to find \(\displaystyle \int-x\sin x\,dx.\)
