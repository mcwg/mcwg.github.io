Solution 3.4.1.

Choice (b) is more manageable.

  1. Given \(u=e^{2x}\text{,}\) \(v'=x\) we have \(u'=2e^{2x}\text{,}\) \(v=0.5x^{2}\text{,}\) so

    \begin{align*} \int\underbrace{e^{2x}}_{u}\underbrace{x}_{v'}\,dx\amp=\underbrace{e^{2x}}_{u}\underbrace{0.5x^2}_{v}-\int\underbrace{2e^{2x}}_{u'}\underbrace{0.5x^{2}}_{v}\,dx \\ \text{so}\quad\int xe^{2x}\,dx\amp=0.5x^2e^{2x}-\int x^2e^{2x}\,dx. \end{align*}

    Notice that the integral on the right is less manageable than the integral we started with.

  2. Given \(u=x\text{,}\) \(v'=2e^{2x}\) we have \(u'=1\text{,}\) \(v=e^{2x}\text{,}\) so

    \begin{align*} \int\underbrace{x}_{u}\underbrace{2e^{2x}}_{v'}\,dx\amp=\underbrace{x}_{u}\underbrace{e^{2x}}_{v}-\int\underbrace{1}_{u'}\underbrace{e^{2x}}_{v}\,dx \\ \text{so}\quad\int xe^{2x}\,dx\amp=xe^{2x}-\int e^{2x}\,dx \\ \amp=xe^{2x}-0.5e^{2x}+C. \end{align*}

    Notice that the integral on the right is more manageable than the integral we started with.
