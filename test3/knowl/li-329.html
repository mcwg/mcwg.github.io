Item 3.4.b.

Given \(u=x\text{,}\) \(v'=2e^{2x}\) we have \(u'=1\text{,}\) \(v=e^{2x}\text{,}\) so

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int\underbrace{x}_{u}\underbrace{2e^{2x}}_{v'}\,dx\amp=\underbrace{x}_{u}\underbrace{e^{2x}}_{v}-\int\underbrace{1}_{u'}\underbrace{e^{2x}}_{v}\,dx \\ \text{so}\quad\int xe^{2x}\,dx\amp=xe^{2x}-\int e^{2x}\,dx \\ \amp=xe^{2x}-0.5e^{2x}+C. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Notice that the integral on the right is more manageable than the integral we started with.
