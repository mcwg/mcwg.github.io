Item 3.3.b.

Given \(u=\cos x\text{,}\) \(v'=x\) we have \(u'=-\sin x\text{,}\) \(v=0.5x^{2}\) so

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int\underbrace{\cos x}_{u}\underbrace{x}_{v'}\,dx\amp=\underbrace{\cos x}_{u}\underbrace{0.5x^2}_{v}-\int\underbrace{-\sin x}_{u'}\underbrace{0.5x^2}_{v}\,dx \\ \text{so}\quad \int x\cos x\,dx\amp=0.5x^2\cos x+\int0.5x^2\sin x\,dx. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Notice that the integral on the right is less manageable than the integral we started with.
