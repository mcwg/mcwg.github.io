Item 2.2.4.

Function (iii). We see that:

\begin{align*} \sin2x+2x\cos2x\amp=\frac d{dx}\left(x\sin2x\right) \amp \text{ Product rule }\\ \int\left(\sin2x+2x\cos2x\right)\,dx\amp=\int\frac d{dx}\left(x\sin2x\right)\,dx\\ \amp=x\sin2x+C \amp \text{ Fundamental Theorem}\\ \int\sin2x+\,dx+\int2x\cos2x \,dx\amp=x\sin2x+C \\ \int2x\cos2x\,dx\amp=x\sin2x-\int\sin2x\,dx \\ \amp=x\sin2x+\frac12\cos2x +C, \end{align*}

so knowing \(\displaystyle\frac d{dx}\left(x\sin2x\right)\) helps us to find \(\displaystyle \int2x\cos2x\,dx.\)
