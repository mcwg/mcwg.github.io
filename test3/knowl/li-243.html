Item 3.3.1.

Given \(u=x,\ v'=\cos x\text{,}\) we have \(u'=1,\ v=\sin x\) so

\begin{align*} \int\underbrace{x}_{u}\underbrace{\cos x}_{v'}\,dx\amp=\underbrace{x}_{u}\underbrace{\sin x}_{v}-\int\underbrace{1}_{u'}\underbrace{\sin x}_{v}\,dx \\ \text{so}\quad \int x\cos x\,dx\amp=x-\sin x-\int\sin x\,dx =x\sin x-\cos x+C. \end{align*}

Notice that the integral on the right is more manageable than the integral we started with.
