This matches expression (iii). We have \(u=x\text{,}\) \(v=e^{3x}\text{,}\) so \(u'=1\text{,}\) \(v'=3e^{3x}\text{,}\) which gives:

\begin{align*} \int \underbrace{x}_{u}\underbrace{3e^{3x}}_{v'}\,dx\amp = \underbrace{x}_{u}\underbrace{e^{3x}}_{v}-\int\underbrace{1}_{u'}\underbrace{e^{3x}}_{v}\,dx\\ \text{so}\quad \int3xe^{3x}\,dx\amp =uv-\int e^{3x}\,dx. \end{align*}
