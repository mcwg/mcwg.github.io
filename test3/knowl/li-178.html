Item 2.2.3.

Function (ii). We see that:

\begin{align*} \cos2x-2x\cos2x\amp=\frac d{dx}\left(x\cos2x\right) \amp \text{ Product rule }\\ \int\left(\cos2x-2x\cos2x\right)\,dx\amp=\int\frac d{dx}\left(x\cos2x\right)\,dx\\ \amp=x\cos2x+C \amp \text{ Fundamental Theorem}\\ \int\cos2x\,dx-\int2x\cos2x\,dx\amp=x\cos2x+C \\ \int-2x\cos2x\,dx\amp=x\cos2x-\int\cos2x \,dx \\ \amp=x\cos2x-\frac12\sin2x + C, \end{align*}

so knowing \(\displaystyle\frac d{dx}\left(x\cos2x\right)\) helps us to find \(\displaystyle \int-2x\sin2x\,dx.\)
