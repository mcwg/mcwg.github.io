Item 3.2.b.

This matches expression (i). We have \(u=2x\text{,}\) \(v=\sin x\) so \(u'=2\text{,}\) \(v'=\cos x\) which gives:

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int \underbrace{2x}_{u}\underbrace{\cos x}_{v'}\,dx\amp = \underbrace{2x}_{u}\underbrace{\sin x}_{v}-\int\underbrace{2}_{u'}\underbrace{\sin x}_{v}\,dx\\ \text{so}\quad\int2x\cos x\,dx\amp =uv-\int2\sin x\,dx \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
