We see that we have written an unfamiliar integral, \(\displaystyle \int\sin\left(x^{3}\right)\cdot3x^{2}\,dx,\) in terms of an even less manageable integral than we started with. In this case, it would be more productive to try the method of substitution, letting \(w=x^{3}\) and \(dw=3x^{2}\,dx\text{.}\)
