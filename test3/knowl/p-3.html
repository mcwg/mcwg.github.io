We can use the product rule to find certain integrals. For instance, according to the product rule,

\begin{equation*} e^x+xe^x=\frac{d}{dx}(xe^x). \end{equation*}

By the Fundamental Theorem, this means that \(e^x+xe^x\) can be integrated:

\begin{equation*} \int(e^x+xe^x)\,dx=xe^x+C. \end{equation*}
