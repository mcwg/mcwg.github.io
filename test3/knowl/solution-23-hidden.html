Choice (a) is more manageable.

  1. Given \(u=x,\ v'=\cos x\text{,}\) we have \(u'=1,\ v=\sin x\) so

    \begin{align*} \int\underbrace{x}_{u}\underbrace{\cos x}_{v'}\,dx\amp=\underbrace{x}_{u}\underbrace{\sin x}_{v}-\int\underbrace{1}_{u'}\underbrace{\sin x}_{v}\,dx \\ \text{so}\quad \int x\cos x\,dx\amp=x-\sin x-\int\sin x\,dx =x\sin x-\cos x+C. \end{align*}

    Notice that the integral on the right is more manageable than the integral we started with.

  2. Given \(u=\cos x\text{,}\) \(v'=x\) we have \(u'=-\sin x\text{,}\) \(v=0.5x^{2}\) so

    \begin{align*} \int\underbrace{\cos x}_{u}\underbrace{x}_{v'}\,dx\amp=\underbrace{\cos x}_{u}\underbrace{0.5x^2}_{v}-\int\underbrace{-\sin x}_{u'}\underbrace{0.5x^2}_{v}\,dx \\ \text{so}\quad \int x\cos x\,dx\amp=0.5x^2\cos x+\int0.5x^2\sin x\,dx. \end{align*}

    Notice that the integral on the right is less manageable than the integral we started with.
