Item 3.2.d.

This matches expression (ii). We have \(u=x\text{,}\) \(v=\sin2x\) so \(u'=1\text{,}\) \(v'=2\cos2x\) which gives:

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int \underbrace{x}_{u}\underbrace{2\cos 2x}_{v'}\,dx\amp = \underbrace{x}_{u}\underbrace{\sin2x}_{v}-\int\underbrace{1}_{u'}\underbrace{\sin2x}_{v}\,dx\\ \text{so}\quad\int2x\cos2x\,dx\amp =uv-\int\sin2x\,dx. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
