Item 3.5.1.

Given \(u=e^x\text{,}\) \(v'=x^2\) we have \(u'=e^x\text{,}\) \(v=(1/3)x^3\text{,}\) so

\begin{align*} \int\underbrace{e^x}_{u}\underbrace{x^2}_{v'}\,dx\amp=\underbrace{e^x}_{u}\underbrace{(1/3)x^3}_{v}-\int\underbrace{e^x}_{u'}\underbrace{(1/3)x^3}_{v}\,dx \\ \text{so}\quad\int x^2e^x\,dx\amp=\frac13x^3e^x-\int\frac13x^3e^x\,dx. \end{align*}

Notice that the integral on the right is less manageable than the integral we started with.
