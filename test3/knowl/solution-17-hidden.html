1. Function (i). We see that:

    \begin{align*} \cos x-x\sin x\amp=\frac d{dx}\left(x\cos x\right) \amp \text{ Product rule }\\ \int\left(\cos x-x\sin x\right)\,dx\amp=\int\frac d{dx}\left(x\cos x\right)\,dx\\ \amp=x\cos x+C \amp \text{ Fundamental Theorem}\\ \int\cos x\,dx-\int x\sin x\,dx\amp=x\cos x+C \\ \int-x\sin x\,dx\amp=x\cos x-\int\cos x\,dx \\ \amp=x\cos x-\sin x+C, \end{align*}

    so knowing \(\displaystyle\frac d{dx}\left(x\cos x\right)\) helps us to find \(\displaystyle \int-x\sin x\,dx.\)

  2. Function (iv). We see that:

    \begin{align*} 2\cos x-2x\sin x\amp=\frac d{dx}\left(2x\cos x\right) \amp \text{ Product rule }\\ \int\left(2\cos x-2x\sin x\right)\,dx\amp=\int\frac d{dx}\left(2x\cos x\right)\,dx\\ \amp=2x\cos x+C \amp \text{ Fundamental Theorem}\\ \int2\cos x\,dx-\int 2x\sin x\,dx\amp=2x\cos x+C \\ \int-2x\sin x\,dx\amp=2x\cos x-\int2\cos x\,dx \\ \amp=2x\cos x-2\sin xC, \end{align*}

    so knowing \(\displaystyle\frac d{dx}\left(2x\cos x\right)\) helps us to find \(\displaystyle \int-2x\sin x\,dx.\)

  3. Function (ii). We see that:

    \begin{align*} \cos2x-2x\cos2x\amp=\frac d{dx}\left(x\cos2x\right) \amp \text{ Product rule }\\ \int\left(\cos2x-2x\cos2x\right)\,dx\amp=\int\frac d{dx}\left(x\cos2x\right)\,dx\\ \amp=x\cos2x+C \amp \text{ Fundamental Theorem}\\ \int\cos2x\,dx-\int2x\cos2x\,dx\amp=x\cos2x+C \\ \int-2x\cos2x\,dx\amp=x\cos2x-\int\cos2x \,dx \\ \amp=x\cos2x-\frac12\sin2x + C, \end{align*}

    so knowing \(\displaystyle\frac d{dx}\left(x\cos2x\right)\) helps us to find \(\displaystyle \int-2x\sin2x\,dx.\)

  4. Function (iii). We see that:

    \begin{align*} \sin2x+2x\cos2x\amp=\frac d{dx}\left(x\sin2x\right) \amp \text{ Product rule }\\ \int\left(\sin2x+2x\cos2x\right)\,dx\amp=\int\frac d{dx}\left(x\sin2x\right)\,dx\\ \amp=x\sin2x+C \amp \text{ Fundamental Theorem}\\ \int\sin2x+\,dx+\int2x\cos2x \,dx\amp=x\sin2x+C \\ \int2x\cos2x\,dx\amp=x\sin2x-\int\sin2x\,dx \\ \amp=x\sin2x+\frac12\cos2x +C, \end{align*}

    so knowing \(\displaystyle\frac d{dx}\left(x\sin2x\right)\) helps us to find \(\displaystyle \int2x\cos2x\,dx.\)
