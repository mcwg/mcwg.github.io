Item 2.2.b.

Function (iv). We see that:

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} 2\cos x-2x\sin x\amp=\frac d{dx}\left(2x\cos x\right) \amp \text{ Product rule }\\ \int\left(2\cos x-2x\sin x\right)\,dx\amp=\int\frac d{dx}\left(2x\cos x\right)\,dx\\ \amp=2x\cos x+C \amp \text{ Fundamental Theorem}\\ \int2\cos x\,dx-\int 2x\sin x\,dx\amp=2x\cos x+C \\ \int-2x\sin x\,dx\amp=2x\cos x-\int2\cos x\,dx \\ \amp=2x\cos x-2\sin xC, \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

so knowing \(\displaystyle\frac d{dx}\left(2x\cos x\right)\) helps us to find \(\displaystyle \int-2x\sin x\,dx.\)
