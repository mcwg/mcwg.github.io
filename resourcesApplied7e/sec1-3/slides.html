Arctic Sea Ice Extent

Let \(E(x)\) and \(F(t)\) be the Arctic Sea ice extent, both in millions of square kilometers, as a function of time, \(x\), in years, since February 2000, and time, \(t\), in days, since January 1, 2019.

Interpret the expression in terms of Arctic Sea ice extent, the area of sea covered by ice.

  1. \(E(19)=14.4\)

  2. \(E(9)=14.8\)

  3. \(\displaystyle{\frac{E(19)-E(9)}{19-9}=-0.04}\)

  4. \(F(31)=14.11\)

  5. \(\displaystyle{\frac{F(59)-F(31)}{59-31}=0.015}\)

Average Rate of Change Geometrically - Part 1

The figure shows the number of farms in the US (in millions) between 1940 and 2020.

  1. Show the quantities (i)–(iv) on the graph of the function in the figure:

    1. 2.8 million farms in 1970

    2. 2.0 million farms in 1990

    3. \(\Delta N\), the change in the number of farms from 1970 to 1990

    4. \(\Delta t\), the change in years from 1970 to 1990

  2. Draw a line whose slope is \(\Delta N/\Delta t\), the average rate of change from 1970 to 1990.

Industrial Pollutants and Pelican Egg Shells

Polychlorinated biphenyl (PCB) is an industrial pollutant thought dangerous to wildlife. The table shows data on PCB levels and the thickness of pelican egg shells. (Thin shells break more easily.)

Find the average rate of change in the thickness of the shell as the PCB concentration changes from 87 ppm to 452 ppm. Give units and explain what the sign of the answer tells us.

Gap Net Sales

The table gives the net sales of The Gap, a clothing retailer. 1

  1. Find the change in net sales between 2017 and 2019.

  2. Find the average rate of change in net sales between 2015 and 2019. Give units and interpret your answer.

  3. From 2015 to 2019, were there any one-year intervals during which the average rate of change was negative? If so, when?
