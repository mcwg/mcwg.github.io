Section 4 Second set: Evaluating expressions that use logs

By the definitions, one can compute exact values of \(\log(y)\) when \(y\) is a power of \(10\text{,}\) and exact values \(\ln(y)\) when \(y\) is a power of \(e\text{.}\)

What is important in the exercises below is that you get practice using the definitions, not the values you find!

Make sure you learn how to use your favorite technological tool to check your answers.

Subsection 4.2 Wrap-Up

  1. Can the log of a number be larger than \(100\text{?}\) Why or why not?
  2. Can the log of a number be smaller than \(0\text{?}\) Why or why not?
  3. Can you evaluate the log of any number? Are there any restrictions? Why or why not?
  4. Which logs are easy to compute without a calculator for \(\log\text{?}\) For \(\ln\text{?}\)

If you are having trouble with these questions, go over the solutions or discuss with your classmates. If neither helps, ask your instructor.
