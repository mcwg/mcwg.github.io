Section 2 Summary of Section

Logarithms.

  • Common log:

    • \(y=\log x\) means \(10^y=x\text{.}\)
    • \(\log 10=1, \log 1=0\text{.}\)

  • Natural log:

    • \(y=\ln x\) means \(e^y=x\text{.}\)
    • \(\ln e=1, \ln 1=0\text{.}\)
Properties of logarithms.
\begin{align*} \log (ab)\amp= \log a+\log b \amp\qquad \ln(ab) \amp = \ln a + \ln b.\\ \log(a/b) \amp= \log a -\log b \amp\qquad \ln(a/b) \amp = \ln a - \ln b.\\ \log (b^t) \amp= t\log b \amp\qquad \ln (b^t) \amp = t\ln b.\\ \log (10^x) \amp= 10^{\log x}= x \amp\qquad \ln (e^x) \amp = e^{\ln x} = x. \end{align*}

Properties of logarithms are related to properties of exponents. For example,

\begin{equation*} \frac{10^n}{10^m}=10^{n-m} \end{equation*}

corresponds to

\begin{equation*} \log\left(\frac{a}{b}\right)=\log a -\log b, \end{equation*}

(one can see this by simplifying \(10^{\log a-\log b}\)).

Similarly \(\left(10^n\right)^m=10^{nm}\) is related to \(\log b^t=t\log b\text{.}\) Regretfully, the relationship is not as transparent to allow one to avoid memorizing the properties of logarithms. One must get lots of practice. There are too many false equations, like \(\log A \log B=\log A+\log B\) (which is false!) that look similar to true properties.

Logarithms to other bases.
  • \(y=\log_bx\) means \(b^y=x\text{.}\)
Base conversion formula.
\begin{equation*} \log_b(x)= \frac{\log x}{\log b} \quad\text{and}\quad \log_bx = \frac{\ln x}{\ln b}. \end{equation*}
