What makes logs really useful for solving equations are the properties

\begin{equation*} \log (b^t)= t\log b \quad\text{and}\quad \ln (b^t) = t\ln b. \end{equation*}

These allow you to bring the variable in an equation “down from an exponent” to solve and equation. You just need to apply logs to both sides of the equation (if two things are equal, then their logs are equal too!).
