Solution 7.3.1.

Since the goal is to get \(t\) by itself as much as possible, first divide both sides by \({5}\text{,}\) and then use logs.

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} {5}({1.063})^t \amp= {24}\\ {1.063}^t \amp= \frac{{24}}{{5}}\\ \log{({1.063})^t} \amp= \log(\frac{{24}}{{5}})\\ t\log{1.063} \amp= \log(\frac{{24}}{{5}})\\ t \amp= \frac{\log(\frac{{24}}{{5}})}{\log{1.063}}={25.675} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
