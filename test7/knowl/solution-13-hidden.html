1. We are looking for a power of \(10\text{,}\) and \(\log{10000}\) is asking for the power of \(10\) which gives \({10000}\text{.}\) Since \(10^{4}= {10000}\text{,}\) we know that \(\log{10000}={4}\text{.}\)

  2. Using the rules of logarithms, we have \(\log\sqrt{{10000}}=\log {10000}^{1/2}=\frac 12\log {10000}=\frac 12\cdot {4}= \frac{{4}}{2}\text{.}\)

  3. We are looking for a power of \(10\text{,}\) and \(\log1\) is asking for the power of \(10\) which gives \(1\text{.}\) Since \(1=10^0\text{,}\) \(\log1=0\text{.}\)

  4. We are looking for a power of \(10\text{,}\) and \(\log{0.001}\) is asking for the power of \(10\) which gives \({0.001}\text{.}\) Since \({0.001}=\frac{1}{10}=10^{-{3}}\text{,}\) we know that \(\log{0.001}=\log10^{-{3}}=-{3}\text{.}\)
