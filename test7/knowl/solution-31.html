Solution 7.4.1.

Since the goal is to get \(t\) by itself as much as possible, first divide both sides by \({96}\text{,}\) and then use logs.

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} {96}({0.74})^t \amp= {78}\\ {0.74}^t \amp= \frac{{78}}{{96}}\\ \log{({0.74})^t} \amp= \log(\frac{{78}}{{96}})\\ t\log{0.74} \amp= \log(\frac{{78}}{{96}})\\ t \amp= \frac{\log(\frac{{78}}{{96}})}{\log{0.74}}={0.69} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
