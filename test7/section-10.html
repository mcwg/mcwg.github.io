Section 7 Fifth set: Using the properties of logarithms to solve more complex exponential equations using logs

The properties \(\log (b^t)= t\log b\) and \(\ln (b^t) = t\ln b\) allow you to bring the variable in an equation “down from an exponent”. This is the basic property that makes logs useful for solving equations. You just need to apply logs to both sides of the equation (if two things are equal, then their logs are equal too!).
