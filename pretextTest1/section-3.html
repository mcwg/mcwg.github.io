Section 3 The method of substitution: first examples

Subsection 3.1 (**) PENDING

INTRO TEXT - EXAMPLE using \(w\)-substitution as a method.

NOTE: Ask students to repeat the same examples from before, but this time with \(w\)-substitution (ask for \(w(x)\text{,}\) \(dw\text{,}\) and the rewrite of the integral --- see DBA which has a substitution checker.

NOTE: Have at least some where the outside and inside functions are hard to recognize, for example \(\int x/(1+x^2)\,dx\) (let \(w=1+x^2\)).

NOTE: Encourage your students not to rely too much on the mechanics of substitution; do examples to convince them that they really can guess-and-check in simple cases. Encourage them to look for patterns. For example, \(\int e^{2x}\,dx=e^{2x}/2+C\) and \(\int\sin(3x)\,dx =-\cos(3x)/3+C\) can be done without substitution.

NOTE: An interesting example is \(\int1/(2x)\,dx\text{.}\) The answer is either \(\frac12\ln x+C\) or \(\frac12\ln(2x)+C\) (using \(w=2x\)). Show that these two antiderivatives differ only by a constant.

WRAP-UP for this piece --- simple straightforward activity.
