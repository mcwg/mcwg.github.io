NOTE: Be sure to demonstrate for students the two different ways of computing a definite integral with respect to \(x\) using substitution. One involves computing the indefinite integral using substitution, writing the antiderivative in terms of \(x\text{,}\) then applying the appropriate limits of integration. The other involves computing the antiderivative in terms of \(w\) and using the relationship between \(x\) and \(w\) to write the limits of integration in terms of \(w\text{.}\)
