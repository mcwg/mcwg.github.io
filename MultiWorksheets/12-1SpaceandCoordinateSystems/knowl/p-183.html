Since we are fixing \(r=3\) and allowing \(\theta\) to vary, this graph is a circle of radius \(3\) centered at the origin. Since \(r=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}\text{,}\) converting to Cartesian coordinates, we get \(\sqrt{x^2+y^2}=3 \text{,}\) or \(x^2+y^2=9 \text{,}\) confirming that this is indeed a circle centered at the origin of radius \(3 \text{.}\) This matches Figure A.
