Solution 4.7.1.

In order to find the center and radius, we need to complete the square in all three variables. We have

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} x^2+y^2+z^2-{6}x+{6}y-{2}z \amp = {17} \\ (x-{3})^2-{9}+(y+{3})^2-{9}+(z-{1})^2-{1} \amp = {17} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Next we rewrite by subtracting all the constant terms to the right hand side giving

\begin{equation*} (x-{3})^2+(y+{3})^2+(z-{1})^2={36}={6}^2. \end{equation*}

Thus this equation is for a sphere whose center is \(({3},{-3},{1})\) and whose radius is \({6}\text{.}\)
