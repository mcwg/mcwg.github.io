  • The point \(({1},{2},{-1})\) does not lie on the graph of \(x=y-z\) since \({1} \neq {2} -({-1})\text{.}\)

  • The point \(({1},{-5},{-6})\) does lie on the graph of \(x=y-z\) since \({1} = {-5} -({-6})\text{.}\)

  • The point \(({-4},{4},{0})\) does not lie on the graph of \(x=y-z\) since \({-4} \neq {4} -({0})\text{.}\)
