Any point in the \(xz\)-plane must have \(y\)-coordinate equal to \(0\text{.}\) Therefore, the closest point in the \(xz\)-plane to the point \((-6,-2,2)\) must be \((-6, 0,2) \text{.}\) Since only the \(y\)-coordinate has changed, these points will be a distance \({2}\) apart. Thus, the point \((-6, -2, 2)\) is a distance of \({2}\) from the \(xz\)-plane.
