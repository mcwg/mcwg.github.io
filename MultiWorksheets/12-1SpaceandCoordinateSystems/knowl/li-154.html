Item 5.1.c.

Since we are fixing \(\theta =\pi /4\) and allowing \(r\geq 0\) to vary, this graph is half of the line \(y=x\) (which makes an angle \(\pi /4\) with the \(x\) -axis) starting at the origin in the first quadrant. Since \(\theta =\pi /4\text{,}\) we have \(\tan (\theta )=y/x\text{,}\) using the fact that \(\theta =\pi /4\text{,}\) we can convert to Cartesian coordinates as \(\tan (\pi /4 )=1=y/x\text{,}\) and so \(y=x\text{.}\)
