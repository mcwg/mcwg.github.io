The graph of a function of a single variable is represented in \(\displaystyle 2 \)-dimensional space, with one axis (usually the \(\displaystyle x \)-axis) giving values of the independent variable, and the other axis (usually the \(\displaystyle y \)-axis) giving values of the dependent variable. In multivariable calculus, we consider functions of two or more variables.
