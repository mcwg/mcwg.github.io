Item 4.6.b.

For \(x=0\text{,}\) we get a vertical plane parallel to the \(yz\)-plane, for \(y=0\) and \(y={1}\text{,}\) we get two vertical planes parallel to the \(xz\)-plane, and for \(z=0\) and \(z={1}\) we get two horizontal planes. The last graph \(x=y\) is a slanted plane whose \(y\) -values increase as \(x\) increases. It follows that the solid region \(0\leq x\leq y\text{,}\) \(0\leq y\leq {1}\text{,}\) \(0\leq z\leq {1}\) is in the shape of a box with a slanted vertical side and all other sides parallel to the three coordinate planes.
