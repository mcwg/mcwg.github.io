For \(x=0\) and \(x={2}\text{,}\) we get two vertical planes parallel to the \(yz\)-plane, for \(y=0\text{,}\) we get a vertical plane parallel to the \(xz\)-plane, and for \(z=0\) and \(z={2}\) we get two horizontal planes. The last graph \(y=z\) is a slanted plane whose \(z\) -values increase as \(y\) increases. It follows that the solid region \(0\leq x\leq {2}\text{,}\) \(0\leq y\leq z\text{,}\) \(0\leq z\leq {2}\) is in the shape of a box with a slanted vertical side and all other sides parallel to the three coordinate planes.
