Next, since \(0\leq \theta \leq 2\pi \text{,}\) this means that \(\theta\) takes on all possible angles. For the lower bound \(r\leq z\text{,}\) we consider the surface \(r=z\text{.}\) Observe that for the surface \(r=z\text{,}\) if we fix the angle \(\theta=0\text{,}\) we see that \(z=r\) is a half-line starting at the origin in the \(xz\)-plane at an angle \(\pi /4\) from the positive \(x\)-axis. Since there are no restrictions on \(\theta\text{,}\) the graph of \(r=z\) is obtained by rotating this line around the \(z\)-axis. In particular, the graph of \(z=r\) is an upward-facing cone with vertex at the origin.
