Item 5.6.a.

First, since \(0\leq \rho\leq {2}\text{,}\) this region occurs inside the sphere of radius \({2}\) centered at the origin. Next, the surface \(\phi =\pi /4\) is a cone centered on the \(z\)-axis, so the bounds \(0\leq \phi \leq \pi/4\) restrict to all points above the cone. Putting this all together, since there are no restrictions on \(\theta\text{,}\) we get a solid cone topped by the solid sphere of radius \({2}\) centered at the origin. This matches Figure D.
