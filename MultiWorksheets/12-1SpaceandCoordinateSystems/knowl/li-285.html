Item 5.5.a.

For a point \(P\) to have a coordinate \(\phi =\pi /4\text{,}\) the line connecting the point to the origin has to make an angle of \(\pi /4\) with the positive \(z\)-axis. Thus the surface \(\phi =\pi /4\) is the set of all points which share this property, which is geometrically an upward-facing cone with vertex at the origin. Converting, since \(z=\rho \cos (\phi)\text{,}\) and \(\rho =\sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}\text{,}\) and \(\phi =\pi /4\text{,}\) we get

\begin{equation*} z=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}. \end{equation*}

Squaring both sides we get

\begin{equation*} z^2=\frac{2x^2+2y^2+2z^2}{4} \end{equation*}

which simplifies to

\begin{equation*} z^2=x^2+y^2. \end{equation*}

Since \(\phi =\pi /4\text{,}\) we must have \(z\geq 0\text{,}\) so we get \(z=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}\) which is a Cartesian equation for a cone. This matches Figure B.
