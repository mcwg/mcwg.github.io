First, if we fix the angle \(\theta=0\) we see that \(z=r\) is a half-line starting at the origin in the \(xz\)-plane at an angle \(\pi /4\) from the positive \(x\)-axis. Since there are no restrictions on \(\theta\) the graph of \(r=z\) is obtained by rotating this line around the \(z\)-axis. In particular, the graph of \(z=r\) is an upward-facing cone with vertex at the origin. Converting, we get \(z=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}\) which is a Cartesian equation for a cone. This matches Figure B.
