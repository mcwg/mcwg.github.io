The vectors in \(\vec{F}\) appear to induce rotation around the \(z\)-axis because, even though they are pointing in the same direction close to the \(z\)-axis, they are larger for \(x\lt 0\) than for \(x>0\text{.}\) Applying the right-hand rule in the direction of the induced rotation, we find that \(\text{curl}\,\vec{F}(0,0,0)\) points in the direction of \(-\vec{k}\text{.}\)
