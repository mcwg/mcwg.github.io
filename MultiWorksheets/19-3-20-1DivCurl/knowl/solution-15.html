Solution 5.5.1.

  1. The vectors in \(\vec{F}\) appear to rotate around the \(x\)-axis. Applying the right-hand rule in the direction of rotation, we get \(\text{curl}\,\vec{F}(0,0,0)\) points in the direction of \(\vec{i}\text{.}\)

  2. Since there is no flow of the vectors in \(\vec{F}\) into or out of the origin, we have \({\rm div} (\vec{F})(0,0,0)=0\text{.}\)
