First, \(S\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(u,v)=\sqrt{4-v}\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +\sqrt{4-v} \sin{(u)} \vec{j} +v\vec{k} \end{equation*}

where \(0\leq u\leq 2\pi\) and \(0\leq v\leq 4\text{,}\) the upper bound coming from the fact that \(z\leq 4\text{.}\) The normal vector is

\begin{equation*} \vec{N} =\sqrt{4-v}\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +\sqrt{4-v} \sin{(u)} \vec{j} +\frac{1}{2}\vec{k}. \end{equation*}

Since the \(\vec{k}\) component of \(\vec{N}\) is positive, this parameterization is orientation preserving. Thus we have

\begin{align*} \int \int_{S} \amp \left( x\vec{i}+y\vec{j} +\vec{k} \right) \cdot \, d\vec{S} =\\ \amp =\int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{4} \left( \sqrt{4-v}\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +\sqrt{4-v} \sin{(u)} \vec{j} +\vec{k} \right)\\ \amp \qquad\quad \cdot \left( \sqrt{4-v}\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +\sqrt{4-v} \sin{(u)} \vec{j} +\frac{1}{2}\vec{k}\right) \,dv\,du\\ \amp =\int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{4} \left( (4-v)\cos^2{(u)} +(4-v) \sin^2{(u)} +\frac{1}{2} \right) \,dv\,du\\ \amp =\int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{4} \left(\frac{9}{2}-v\right) \,dv\,du\\ \amp =2\pi \left( \frac{9v}{2} -\frac{v^2}{2} \right)_{0}^{4}\\ \amp = 20\pi \end{align*}
