Solution 5.4.1.

First, \(S\) can be parameterized by \(\vec{r}(u,v)=u\vec{i} +v\vec{j}\) over the region \(D\) with \(u^2+v^2\leq 5\text{.}\) The normal vector is \(\vec{N} =\vec{k}\) and so \(\vec{r}(u,v)\) is orientation preserving. Thus we have

\begin{align*} \int \int_{S} \amp \left( (y-x)\vec{i} +(z-y)\vec{j} +(x-z)\vec{k} \right) \cdot \, d\vec{S} =\\ \amp = \int \int_{D} \left( (v-u)\vec{i} +(0-v)\vec{j} +(u-0)\vec{k} \right) \cdot \vec{k} \,dv\,du\\ \amp =\int \int_{D} u \,dv\,du \end{align*}

Since the region \(D\) is the circle \(u^2+v^2\leq 5\text{,}\) we convert this double integral to polar coordinates with \(u=r\cos{(\theta)}\text{,}\) \(v=r\sin{(\theta)}\text{,}\) \(0\leq r\leq \sqrt{5}\) and \(0\leq \theta \leq 2\pi\text{.}\) Then we have

\begin{align*} \int \int_{D} u \,dv\,du \amp =\int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{\sqrt{5}} r\cos{(\theta)} r\, dr\, d\theta \\ \amp = \left( \int_{0}^{2\pi} \cos{(\theta)} \, d\theta\right) \left( \int_{0}^{\sqrt{5}} r^2 \, dr\right) \\ \amp = \left( \sin{(\theta)} \right)_{0}^{2\pi} \left( \frac{r^3}{3} \right)_{0}^{\sqrt{5}} \\ \amp = 0. \end{align*}
