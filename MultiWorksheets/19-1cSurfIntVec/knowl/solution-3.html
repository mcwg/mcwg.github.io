Solution 5.1.1.

First, \(S\) can be parameterized by \(\vec{r}(u,v)=u\vec{i} +v\vec{j} +{4}\vec{k}\text{,}\) where \(0\leq v\leq 1\) and \(0\leq u\leq 1\text{.}\) The normal vector to this parameterization is \(\vec{N} =\vec{k}\text{,}\) so this parameterization is orientation-preserving. Thus, we have:

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \int \int_{S} (x^2\vec{i}+yz\vec{j})\, \cdot d\vec{S} \amp =\int_{0}^{1} \int_{0}^{1} \left( u^2\vec{i} +{4}v\vec{j} \right) \cdot \vec{k} \,du\,dv\\ \amp =\int_{0}^{1} \int_{0}^{1}0 \,du\,dv\\ \amp = 0. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
