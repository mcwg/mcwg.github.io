Recall that a line integral of a vector field \(\vec{F}\) over a curve \(C\) parameterized by \(\vec{r}(t)\) for \(a\leq t\leq b\) is calculated using the formula

\begin{equation*} \int_{C} \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{S}=\int_{a}^{b} \vec{F}(\vec{r}(t)) \cdot \vec{r}'(t)\,dt. \end{equation*}

Describe the differences and similarities to the surface integral \(\int \int_{S} \vec{F} (x,y,z)\cdot d\vec{S}\text{.}\) That is, describe how the formulas differ or share similarities and how the process of calculation differs or share similarities.
