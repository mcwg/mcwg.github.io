We have \(\vec{r}(0,0)=2\vec{i} =(2,0)\) and \(\vec{N} (0,0)=2\vec{i}\text{.}\) In particular, the vector \(\vec{N}\) at the point \((2,0)\) points out of the cylinder, and thus this parameterization is orientation reversing. Thus we have:

\begin{align*} \int \amp \int_{S} \left( zx\vec{i} +zy\vec{j} +(x^3+y^3)\vec{k} \right) \cdot\, d\vec{S} =\\ \amp = - \int_{0}^{5} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \left( 2v\cos{(u)}\vec{i} +2v\sin{(u)} \vec{j} +(8\cos^3{(u)} +8\sin^3{(u)})\vec{k} \right) \\ \amp \qquad \qquad \quad \cdot \left( 2\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +2\sin{(u)} \vec{j} \right) \,du\,dv\\ \amp =- \int_{0}^{5} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \left( 4v\cos^2{(u)} +4v\sin^2{(u)} \right) \,du\,dv\\ \amp =- \int_{0}^{5} \int_{0}^{2\pi} 4v \,du\,dv\\ \amp =- 4\pi v^2\bigg|_{0}^5 \\ \amp =- 100\pi. \end{align*}
