Solution 5.2.1.

First, \(S\) can be parameterized by \(\vec{r}(u,v)=u\vec{i} +v\vec{j} +(u^2+v^2)\vec{k}\text{.}\) Since we have \(0\leq z\leq 4\text{,}\) this means the corresponding restrictions on \(u\) and \(v\) are \(0\leq u^2+v^2\leq 4\text{,}\) so the region \(D\) within the circle \(u^2+v^2=4\text{.}\) The normal vector is \(\vec{N} =-2u \vec{i} -2v \vec{j} +\vec{k}\text{.}\) Since \(S\) is an upward facing parabolic bowl, the inward pointing normal vector will have positive \(\vec{k}\) component, and thus this parameterization is orientation preserving. Thus we have

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int \int_{S} (x\vec{i}+y\vec{j})\, \cdot d\vec{S} \amp =\int \int_{D} (u\vec{i}+v\vec{j})\cdot \left( -2u \vec{i} -2v \vec{j} +\vec{k} \right) \,du\,dv\\ \amp =\int \int_{D} \left(-2u^2 -2v^2\right) \,du\,dv \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Since the region of integration is the disk \(u^2+v^2\leq 4\text{,}\) we switch to polar coordinates with \(u=r\cos{(\theta)}\text{,}\) \(v=r\sin{(\theta)}\text{,}\) \(0\leq r\leq 2\) and \(0\leq \theta \leq 2\pi\text{.}\) This gives:

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int \int_{D} \left(-2u^2 -2v^2\right) \,du\,dv \amp = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{2} (-2r^2)r\, dr\, d\theta \\ \amp = -4\pi \int_{0}^{2} r^2\, dr \\ \amp = -4\pi \frac{r^4}{4}\big|_{0}^{2} \\ \amp = -16\pi. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
