First we note that since \(S\) is the flat disk \(x^2+y^2\leq 1\) in the plane \(z=-{3}\) oriented upward, its orientation is the vector \(\vec{k}\text{.}\) In particular, only the \(\vec{k}\)-component of vector field \(\vec{F}\) contributes to the flow through \(S\text{.}\)

  1. Since the \(\vec{k}\)-component \(z^2\) of \(\vec{F}\) is positive on the surface \(S\text{,}\) the vector field \(\vec{F}\) flows with the orientation of \(S\text{,}\) and thus this integral is positive.

  2. Since the \(\vec{k}\)-component \(-{5}x^2\) of \(\vec{F}\) is negative on the surface \(S\text{,}\) the vector field \(\vec{F}\) flows against the orientation of \(S\text{,}\) and thus this integral is negative.

  3. Since the \(\vec{k}\)-component \(-{7}z\) of \(\vec{F}\) is positive on the surface \(S\) (since \(z=-{3}\)), the vector field \(\vec{F}\) flows with the orientation of \(S\text{,}\) and thus this integral is positive.

  4. Since the \(\vec{k}\)-component of \(\vec{F}\) is zero on the surface \(S\text{,}\) the vector field \(\vec{F}\) does not flow through \(S\text{,}\) thus this integral is zero.
