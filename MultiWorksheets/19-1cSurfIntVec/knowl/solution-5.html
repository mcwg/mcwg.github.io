Solution 5.3.1.

First, \(S\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(u,v)=\sqrt{{7}-v}\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +\sqrt{{7}-v} \sin{(u)} \vec{j} +v\vec{k}\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(0\leq u\leq 2\pi\) and \(0\leq v\leq {7}\text{,}\) the upper bound coming from the fact that \(z\leq {7}\text{.}\) The normal vector is

\begin{equation*} \vec{N} =\sqrt{{7}-v}\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +\sqrt{{7}-v} \sin{(u)} \vec{j} +\frac{1}{2}\vec{k}. \end{equation*}

Since the \(\vec{k}\) component of \(\vec{N}\) is positive, this parameterization is orientation-preserving. Thus, we have

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \int \int_{S} \amp \left( x\vec{i}+y\vec{j} +{9}\vec{k} \right) \cdot \, d\vec{S} =\\ \amp =\int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{{7}} \left( \sqrt{{7}-v}\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +\sqrt{{7}-v} \sin{(u)} \vec{j} +{9}\vec{k} \right)\\ \amp \qquad\quad \cdot \left( \sqrt{{7}-v}\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +\sqrt{{7}-v} \sin{(u)} \vec{j} +\frac{1}{2}\vec{k}\right) \,dv\,du\\ \amp =\int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{{7}} \left( ({7}-v)\cos^2{(u)} +({7}-v) \sin^2{(u)} +\frac{{9}}{2} \right) \,dv\,du\\ \amp =\int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{{7}} \left(\frac{{23}}{2}-v\right) \,dv\,du\\ \amp =2\pi \left( \frac{{23}v}{2} -\frac{v^2}{2} \right)\Bigg|_{0}^{{7}}\\ \amp = {112}\pi \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
