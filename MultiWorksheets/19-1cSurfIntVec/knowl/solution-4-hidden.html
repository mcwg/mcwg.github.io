First, \(S\) can be parameterized by \(\vec{r}(u,v)=u\vec{i} +v\vec{j} +(u^2+v^2)\vec{k}\text{.}\) Since we have \(0\leq z\leq {25}\text{,}\) this means the corresponding restrictions on \(u\) and \(v\) are \(0\leq u^2+v^2\leq {25}\text{,}\) so the region \(D\) within the circle \(u^2+v^2={25}\text{.}\) The normal vector is \(\vec{N} =-2u \vec{i} -2v \vec{j} +\vec{k}\text{.}\) Since \(S\) is an upward-facing parabolic bowl, the inward-pointing normal vector has positive \(\vec{k}\) component, and thus this parameterization is orientation-preserving. Thus we have

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \int \int_{S} (x\vec{i}+y\vec{j})\, \cdot d\vec{S} \amp =\int \int_{D} (u\vec{i}+v\vec{j})\cdot \left( -2u \vec{i} -2v \vec{j} +\vec{k} \right) \,du\,dv\\ \amp =\int \int_{D} \left(-2u^2 -2v^2\right) \,du\,dv \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Since the region of integration is the disk \(u^2+v^2\leq {25}\text{,}\) we switch to polar coordinates with \(u=r\cos{(\theta)}\text{,}\) \(v=r\sin{(\theta)}\text{,}\) \(0\leq r\leq {5}\) and \(0\leq \theta \leq 2\pi\text{.}\) This gives:

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \int \int_{D} \left(-2u^2 -2v^2\right) \,du\,dv \amp = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{{5}} (-2r^2)r\, dr\, d\theta \\ \amp = -4\pi \int_{0}^{{5}} r^3\, dr \\ \amp = -4\pi \frac{r^4}{4}\big|_{0}^{{5}} \\ \amp = -{625}\pi. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
