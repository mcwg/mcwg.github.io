Solution 5.5.1.

First, \(S\) can be parameterized by \(\vec{r}(u,v)={5}\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +{5}\sin{(u)} \vec{j} +v\vec{k}\text{,}\) with \(0\leq v\leq {6}\) and \(0\leq u\leq 2\pi\text{.}\) The normal vector is

\begin{equation*} \vec{N} ={5}\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +{5}\sin{(u)} \vec{j} \text{.} \end{equation*}

We have \(\vec{N}(0,0)={5}\vec{i}\text{.}\) In particular, the vector \(\vec{N}\) at the point \(({5},0,0)\) points out of the cylinder, and thus this parameterization is orientation-reversing. Therefore, we have:

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \int \amp \int_{S} \left( zx\vec{i} +zy\vec{j} +(x^3+y^3)\vec{k} \right) \cdot\, d\vec{S} =\\ \amp = - \int_{0}^{{6}} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \bigg( {5}v\cos{(u)}\vec{i} +{5}v\sin{(u)} \vec{j} \bigg.\\ \amp \qquad \qquad \quad + \bigg. ({125}\cos^3{(u)} +{125}\sin^3{(u)})\vec{k} \bigg) \\ \amp \qquad \qquad \quad \cdot \left( {5}\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +{5}\sin{(u)} \vec{j} \right) \,du\,dv\\ \amp =- \int_{0}^{{6}} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \left( {25}v\cos^2{(u)} +{25}v\sin^2{(u)} \right) \,du\,dv\\ \amp =- \int_{0}^{{6}} \int_{0}^{2\pi} {25}v \,du\,dv\\ \amp =- {25}\pi v^2\bigg|_{0}^{6} \\ \amp =- {900}\pi. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
