Solution 2.2.7.1.

When we differentiate with respect to \(x\text{,}\) we think of \(y\) as a constant. Thus,

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} f_x(x,y)\amp= \frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left(x^2y-{6}x^2+{12}xy^2\right) \\ \amp= \frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left(x^2y\right)-\frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left({6}x^2\right)+\frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left({12}xy^2\right)\\ \amp = 2xy - {12}x + {12}y^2 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
