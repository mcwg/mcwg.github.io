Solution 5.3.1.

A critical point of a function \(h(x,y)\) is a point where \(\nabla h(x,y)=\vec{0}\) or where \(\nabla h(x,y)\) is undefined. The partial derivatives

\begin{align*} h_x(x,y)\amp = x^2 + x + y^2\\ h_y(x,y)\amp = 2xy+2y , \end{align*}

show that \(\nabla h(x,y)\) is defined everywhere, and \(\nabla h(x,y)=\vec{0}\) only at the points which are solutions to the system of equations

\begin{align*} x^2 + x + y^2 \amp = 0 \\ 2xy+2y \amp = 0. \end{align*}

Factoring out a \(2y\) in the second equation gives \(2y(1+x)=0\text{,}\) so either \(2y=0\) or \(1+x=0\) (or both). This shows that any solution \((x,y)\) to the system must have \(y=0\) or \(x=-1\text{.}\) Setting \(y=0\) in the first equation gives \[x+x^2=0,\] which has solutions \(x=0\) or \(x=\text{.}\) Thus, \((x,y)=(0,0)\) and \((x,y)=(,0)\) are two solutions (the only solutions with \(y=0\)). Setting \(x=-1\) in the first equation gives \(-4+y^2=0\text{,}\) so \(y=\pm 2\text{.}\) Thus, \((x,y)=(-1,-2)\) and \((x,y)=(-1,2)\) are two more solutions (the only solutions with \(x=-1\)).

Therefore, the solutions to the system are \((0,0)\text{,}\) \((,0)\text{,}\) \((-1,-2)\) and \((-1,2)\text{.}\) These are the critical points.

We compute

\begin{align*} D \amp =\det \pmatrix{h_{xx} \amp h_{xy} \\ h_{yx} \amp h_{yy}}\\ \amp =\det \pmatrix{x+ \amp 2y \\ 2y \amp 2x+2}\\ \amp = x^2 + x - 4 y^2 + . \end{align*}

At the point \((0,0)\) we have \(D(0,0)=\gt 0\text{,}\) so \((0,0)\) is a local maximum or minimum. To figure out which one it is, we can look at, for example, the concavity in \(x\)-direction by computing \(h_{xx}(0,0)\) which gives \(h_{xx}(0,0)=\gt 0\text{.}\) This indicates that \(h\) is concave up in the \(x\)-direction at \((0,0)\) and so \((0,0)\) is a local minimum.

At the point \((,0)\) we have \(D(,0)=\gt 0\text{,}\) so \((,0)\) is a local maximum or minimum. We figure out which it is again by computing \(h_{xx}(,0)=\lt 0\text{.}\) This indicates that \(h\) is concave down in the \(x\)-direction at \((,0)\) and so \((,0)\) is a local maximum.

At the points \((-1,-2)\) and \((-1,2)\) we have \(D(-1,-2)=D(-1,2)= \lt 0\text{,}\) and so both are saddle points.
