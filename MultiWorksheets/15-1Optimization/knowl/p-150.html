Regarding the point \((-1,2)\) note that if you move away from the point in the \(-\vec{i}+\vec{j}\) direction (or its direct opposite, the \(\vec{i}-\vec{j}\) direction) the values of the contours seem to decrease. However, if you move away from the point in the \(\vec{i}+\vec{j}\) direction (or its direct opposite, the \(-\vec{i}-\vec{j}\) direction) the values of the contours seem to increase. This indicates that \((-1,2)\) is a saddle point. A similar reasoning shows that \((-1,-2)\) is also a saddle point.
