Solution 2.2.8.1.

When we differentiate with respect to \(y\text{,}\) we think of \(x\) as a constant. Thus,

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} g_y(x,y)\amp =\frac{\partial}{\partial y}\left({6}y-{3}x^2y+{2}xy^2+{6}\right)\\ \amp = \frac{\partial}{\partial y}\left({6}y\right)-\frac{\partial}{\partial y}\left({3}x^2y\right)+\frac{\partial}{\partial y} \left({2}xy^2\right)+\frac{\partial}{\partial y}\left({6}\right)\\ \amp = {6}-{3}x^2+{4}xy \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
