Solution 6.1.1.

Since this surface is the graph of \(z={xy-y^{4}}\text{,}\) we can choose \(x=u\text{,}\) \(y=v\text{,}\) and we have \(z={uv-v^{4}}\text{.}\) Thus

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(u,v) =u\vec{i} +v\vec{j} +({uv-v^{4}})\vec{k} \end{equation*}

is a parameterization for this surface.
