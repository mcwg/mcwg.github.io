Item 6.7.c.

To determine if \(\vec{r}\) is orientation-preserving, it suffices to check whether or not \(\vec{N}\) points outward at a given point. Choosing \(v=1\) and \(u=0\text{,}\) we get the point \(\vec{r}(0,1) =1\cos(0)\vec{i} +1\vec{j} +1\sin(0)\vec{k}=(1,1,0)\text{.}\) Since the cone is centered on the \(y\)-axis, an outward-pointing normal vector at the point \((1,1,0)\) must have a positive \(\vec{i}\) component.

The normal vector from the parameterization \(\vec{r}\) at this point is \(\vec{N} (0,1)=-1\cos(0)\vec{i} +1\vec{j} -1\sin(0)\vec{k} =-\vec{i} +\vec{j}\text{,}\) which has a negative \(\vec{i}\) component. Therefore, this parameterization is orientation-reversing.
