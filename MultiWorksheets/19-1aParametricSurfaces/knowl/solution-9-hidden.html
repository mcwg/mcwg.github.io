Since \(x=\cos(v)\text{,}\) \(y=\sin(v)\text{,}\) and \(z=u\text{,}\) the cross sections of the surface \(S\) are circles centered about the \(z\)-axis whose radius is always equal to \(1\text{.}\) Thus, \(S\) is the graph of the cylinder \(x^2+y^2=1\text{.}\) This can also be verified algebraically since \(x=\cos(v)\) and \(y=\sin(v)\) satisfy the equation \(1=x^2+y^2\text{.}\)
