Solution 5.5.1.

Since \(x=u\cos(v)\text{,}\) \(y=u\sin(v))\text{,}\) and \(z=u\text{,}\) the cross sections of the surface \(S\) are concentric circles about the \(z\)-axis whose radius increases linearly with the height \(z\text{.}\) Thus, \(S\) is the graph of the cone \(z=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}\text{.}\) This can also be verified algebraically since substituting \(x=u\cos(v)\) and \(y=u\sin(v)\) into the equation \(z=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}\) gives \(z=u\text{.}\)
