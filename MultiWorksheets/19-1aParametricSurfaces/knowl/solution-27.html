Solution 7.3.1.
  1. Since \(z=v\) in this parametrization, we have \(0\leq v\leq \text{.}\) We know \(x=\sqrt{-v^2}\cos(u)\) and \(y=\sqrt{-v^2}\sin(u)\text{,}\) so since \(x\leq 0\) we need \(\pi/2 \leq u\leq 3\pi/2\text{.}\)

  2. We have

    \begin{align*} \vec{N} \amp = \vec{r}_{u} \times \vec{r}_{v} \\ \amp = \left( -\sqrt{-v^2}\sin(u)\vec{i} +\sqrt{-v^2}\cos(u)\vec{j}\right) \\ \amp \quad \times \left( -\frac{v}{\sqrt{-v^2}} \cos(u)\vec{i} -\frac{v}{\sqrt{-v^2}} \sin(u)\vec{j} +\vec{k}\right) \\ \amp = \sqrt{-v^2}\cos(u)\vec{i} +\sqrt{-v^2}\sin{(u)}\vec{j} + v\vec{k}. \end{align*}

    As a point of interest, we note that in this case, the normal vector \(\vec{N}\) is the same as the parameterization since the position vectors defining \(\vec{r}(u,v)\) point our radially and normally to the sphere.

  3. Since we have \(\vec{N} =\sqrt{-v^2}\cos(u)\vec{i} +\sqrt{-v^2}\sin{(u)}\vec{j} + v\vec{k}\) and \(v\geq 0\text{,}\) the normal vector \(\vec{N}\) is always pointing up. Therefore this is an orientation preserving parameterization.
