Let \(\vec{u} =a\vec{i} +b\vec{j} +c\vec{k}\text{.}\) Calculating the dot product \(\vec{u} \cdot \vec{k} =c\text{,}\) condition (1) implies that \(c={3}\text{.}\) Calculating the cross product \(\vec{u} \times \vec{k} =b\vec{i}-a\vec{j}\) implies that \(b={8}\) and \(a=-{2}\text{.}\) Therefore, \(\vec{u} =-{2}\vec{i} +{8}\vec{j} +{3}\vec{k}\text{.}\)
