  1. Since \(x=u\text{,}\) \(y=v\) and \(z={6-5u-2v}\text{,}\) in order to be in the first quadrant, we must have all three inequalities \(u\geq 0\text{,}\) \(v\geq 0\) and \({6-5u-2v}\geq 0\) true. This last inequality gives \(v\leq {3-{\textstyle\frac{5}{2}}u}\text{.}\) Sketching the region \(u\geq 0\text{,}\) \(v\geq 0\) and \(v\leq {3-{\textstyle\frac{5}{2}}u}\text{,}\) we get a triangle with corresponding bounds \(0\leq u\leq {{\textstyle\frac{6}{5}}}\) and \(0\leq v\leq {3-{\textstyle\frac{5}{2}}u}\text{,}\) or equivalently \(0\leq v\leq {3}\) and \(0\leq u\leq {{\textstyle\frac{6}{5}}-{\textstyle\frac{2}{5}}v}\text{.}\)

  2. We have

  3. We know \(\vec{N} = {5\boldsymbol{i}+2\boldsymbol{j}+\boldsymbol{k}}\) which has a positive \(\vec{k}\) component so points upward everywhere. It follows that \(\vec{r}\) is orientation-preserving.
