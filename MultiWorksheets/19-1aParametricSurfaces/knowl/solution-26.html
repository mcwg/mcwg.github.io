Solution 7.2.1.

  1. Since \(x=u\text{,}\) \(y=v\) and \(z={3-5u-5v}\text{,}\) in order to be in the first quadrant, we must have all three inequalities \(u\geq 0\text{,}\) \(v\geq 0\) and \({3-5u-5v}\geq 0\) true. This last inequality gives \(v\leq {{\textstyle\frac{3}{5}}-1u}\text{.}\) Sketching the region \(u\geq 0\text{,}\) \(v\geq 0\) and \(v\leq {{\textstyle\frac{3}{5}}-1u}\text{,}\) we get a triangle with corresponding bounds \(0\leq u\leq {{\textstyle\frac{3}{5}}}\) and \(0\leq v\leq {{\textstyle\frac{3}{5}}-1u}\text{,}\) or equivalently \(0\leq v\leq {{\textstyle\frac{3}{5}}}\) and \(0\leq u\leq {{\textstyle\frac{3}{5}}-1v}\text{.}\)

  2. We have

    \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \vec{N} \amp = \vec{r}_{u} \times \vec{r}_{v} \\ \amp = \left( {\boldsymbol{i}-5\boldsymbol{k}} \right) \times \left( {\boldsymbol{j}-5\boldsymbol{k}} \right) \\ \amp = {5\boldsymbol{i}+5\boldsymbol{j}+\boldsymbol{k}}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

  3. We know \(\vec{N} = {5\boldsymbol{i}+5\boldsymbol{j}+\boldsymbol{k}}\) which has a positive \(\vec{k}\) component so points upward everywhere. It follows that \(\vec{r}\) is orientation preserving.
