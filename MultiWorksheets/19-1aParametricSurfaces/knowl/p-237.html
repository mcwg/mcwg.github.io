Since \(x=u\text{,}\) \(y=v\) and \(z={3-5u-5v}\text{,}\) in order to be in the first quadrant, we must have all three inequalities \(u\geq 0\text{,}\) \(v\geq 0\) and \({3-5u-5v}\geq 0\) true. This last inequality gives \(v\leq {{\textstyle\frac{3}{5}}-1u}\text{.}\) Sketching the region \(u\geq 0\text{,}\) \(v\geq 0\) and \(v\leq {{\textstyle\frac{3}{5}}-1u}\text{,}\) we get a triangle with corresponding bounds \(0\leq u\leq {{\textstyle\frac{3}{5}}}\) and \(0\leq v\leq {{\textstyle\frac{3}{5}}-1u}\text{,}\) or equivalently \(0\leq v\leq {{\textstyle\frac{3}{5}}}\) and \(0\leq u\leq {{\textstyle\frac{3}{5}}-1v}\text{.}\)
