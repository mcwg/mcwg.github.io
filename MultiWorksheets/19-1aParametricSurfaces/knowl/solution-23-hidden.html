1. Since \(y=v\) in this parametrization, we have \(0\leq v\leq \text{.}\) We know \(x=v\cos(u)\text{,}\) so since we want \(x\geq 0\text{,}\) we must have \(-\pi/2 \leq u\leq \pi/2\text{.}\)

  2. We have

    \begin{align*} \vec{N} \amp = \vec{r}_{u} \times \vec{r}_{v} \\ \amp = \left( -v\sin(u)\vec{i} +v\cos(u)\vec{k}\right) \times \left( \cos(u)\vec{i} +\vec{j} +\sin(u)\vec{k}\right) \\ \amp = -v\cos(u)\vec{i} +v\vec{j} -v\sin(u)\vec{k}. \end{align*}

  3. To determine if \(\vec{r}\) is orientation preserving, it suffices to check whether or not \(\vec{N}\) points outward at a given point. Choosing \(v=1\) and \(u=0\text{,}\) we get the point \(\vec{r}(0,1) =1\cos(0)\vec{i} +1\vec{j} +1\sin(0)\vec{k}=(1,1,0)\text{.}\) Since the cone is centered on the \(y\)-axis, an outward pointing normal vector at the point \((1,1,0)\) must have a positive \(\vec{i}\) component.

    The normal vector from the parameterization \(\vec{r}\) at this point is \(\vec{N} (0,1)=-1\cos(0)\vec{i} +1\vec{j} -1\sin(0)\vec{k} =-\vec{i} +\vec{j}\text{,}\) which has a negative \(\vec{i}\) component. Therefore, this parameterization is orientation reversing.
