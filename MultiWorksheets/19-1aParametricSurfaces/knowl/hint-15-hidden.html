In a parameterizations \(\vec{r}(u,v) =f(u,v)\vec{i} +g(u,v)\vec{j} +h(u,v)\vec{k}\text{,}\) we have \(x=f(u,v)\text{,}\) \(y=g(u,v)\) and \(z=h(u,v)\text{.}\) Use the given restrictions on \(x\text{,}\) \(y\) and \(z\) and these equations to find the corresponding restrictions on \(u\) and \(v\text{.}\)

  2. Recall that \(\vec{N} =\vec{r}_u\times \vec{r}_{v}\text{.}\)

  3. To determine if \(\vec{r}\) is orientation-preserving, it suffices to check whether it is orientation-preserving at a specific point. Therefore, find \(\vec{N}\) at a specific point and compare to the given orientation. Note: it is often enough to just check the sign at a single component.
