Item 7.3.b.

We have

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \vec{N} \amp = \vec{r}_{u} \times \vec{r}_{v} \\ \amp = \left( -\sqrt{{1}-v^2}\sin(u)\vec{i} +\sqrt{{1}-v^2}\cos(u)\vec{j}\right) \\ \amp \quad \times \left( -\frac{v}{\sqrt{{1}-v^2}} \cos(u)\vec{i} -\frac{v}{\sqrt{{1}-v^2}} \sin(u)\vec{j} +\vec{k}\right) \\ \amp = \sqrt{{1}-v^2}\cos(u)\vec{i} +\sqrt{{1}-v^2}\sin{(u)}\vec{j} + v\vec{k}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

As a point of interest, we note that in this case, the normal vector \(\vec{N}\) is the same as the parameterization since the position vectors defining \(\vec{r}(u,v)\) point our radially and normally to the sphere.
