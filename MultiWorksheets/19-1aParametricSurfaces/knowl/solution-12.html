Solution 6.3.1.

The vectors \(\vec{PQ}=-{6}\vec{i} -\vec{j} -{7}\vec{k}\) and \(\vec{PR} =\vec{i} -\vec{j} -\vec{k}\) are both in the plane. Therefore, letting \(\vec{P} =\vec{i} +{3}\vec{j} +{7}\vec{k}\text{,}\) a parameterization for the plane is

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \vec{r}(u,v) \amp =\vec{P} +u\vec{PQ} +v\vec{PR} \\ \amp =(1-{6}u+v)\vec{i} +({3}-u-v)\vec{j} +({7}-{7}u-v)\vec{k}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
