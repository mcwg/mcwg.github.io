1. Since \(x=u\text{,}\) \(y=v\) and \(z=\sqrt{u^2+v^2}\text{,}\) in order to have \(z\leq {3}\text{,}\) we must have \(\sqrt{u^2+v^2}\leq {3}\) or equivalently \(u^2+v^2\leq {9}\text{.}\) This is all points within the circle \(u^2+v^2\leq {9}\text{,}\) and thus can be bounded by either \(-{3}\leq u\leq {3}\) and \(-\sqrt{{9}-u^2} \leq v\leq \sqrt{{9}-u^2}\text{,}\) or \(-{3}\leq v\leq {3}\) and \(-\sqrt{{9}-v^2} \leq u\leq \sqrt{{9}-v^2}\text{.}\)

  2. We have

    \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \vec{N} \amp = \vec{r}_{u} \times \vec{r}_{v} \\ \amp = \left( \vec{i} +\frac{u}{\sqrt{u^2+v^2}} \vec{k} \right) \times \left( \vec{j} +\frac{v}{\sqrt{u^2+v^2}}\vec{k} \right) \\ \amp = -\frac{u}{\sqrt{u^2+v^2}} \vec{i} -\frac{v}{\sqrt{u^2+v^2}}\vec{j} +\vec{k}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

  3. We know \(\vec{N} =-\frac{u}{\sqrt{u^2+v^2}} \vec{i} -\frac{v}{\sqrt{u^2+v^2}}\vec{j} +\vec{k}\) which has a positive \(\vec{k}\) component so points upward everywhere. It follows that \(\vec{r}\) is orientation reversing.
