Hint 6.6.1.

Recall that if a surface \(S\) has contours which consist of concentric circles (or part of concentric circles) centered on the \(z\)-axis whose radii are a function of height \(f(z)\text{,}\) then \(S\) can be parameterized by letting \(z=v\text{,}\) \(x=f(v)\cos{(u)}\) and \(y=f(v)\sin{(u)}\text{.}\)
