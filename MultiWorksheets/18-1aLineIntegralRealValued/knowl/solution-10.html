Solution 2.2.9.1.

We use the substitution \(u=1-x^2\text{.}\) When \(x=0\text{,}\) we have \(u=1\text{,}\) and when \(x=1\text{,}\) we have \(u=0\text{.}\) We also have \(du=-2xdx\text{,}\) so

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \int_{0}^{1} 2x\sqrt{1-x^2} dx \amp =\int_{1}^{0} -\sqrt{u}du\\ \amp =\int_{0}^{1} \sqrt{u}du =\frac{2}{3} u^{\frac{3}{2}}\bigg|_{0}^{1}\\ \amp =\frac{2}{3} \left(1\right)^{\frac{3}{2}}-\frac{2}{3} \left(0\right)^{\frac{3}{2}}\\ \amp =\frac{2}{3}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
