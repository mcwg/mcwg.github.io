The upper half of the circle of radius \({5}\) from \(({5},0)\) to \((-{5},0)\) can be parametrized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(t)=({5}\cos t) \vec{i}+({5}\sin t)\vec{j},\quad 0\leq t\leq \pi. \end{equation*}

Then we have \(\vec{r}'(t)=(-{5}\sin{t}) \vec{i} + ({5}\cos{t})\vec{j}\) and so

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \int_{C} y ds \amp = \int_0^{\pi} \left({5}\sin t \right) \sqrt{(-{5}\sin t)^2+({5}\cos t)^2}dt\\ \amp = \int_0^{\pi} \left({5}\sin t \right) \sqrt{{25}}dt\\ \amp = {25}\left( -\cos t \right)\bigg|_{0}^{\pi} \\ \amp = {50}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
