Solution 3.3.1.

The line segment from \(({5},{1})\) to \(({6},-{3})\) can be parametrized by \[\vec{r}(t)=(1t+5)\vec{i}+(-4t+1)\vec{j},\quad 0\leq t\leq 1.\] Then we have \(\vec{r}'(t)={1}\vec{i} -{4}\vec{j}\) and so

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \displaystyle\int_{C} (x-y) ds \amp = \int_0^{1} \left( ({1}t+{5})-(-{4}t+{1})\right) \sqrt{({1})^2+(-{4})^2} dt \\ \amp = \int_0^{1} ({5}t+{4}) \sqrt{{17}} dt\\ \amp = \sqrt{{17}} \left( \frac{{5}t^2}{2} +{4}t\right)\bigg|_{0}^{1}\\ \amp = {{\textstyle\frac{13}{2}}} \sqrt{{17}}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
