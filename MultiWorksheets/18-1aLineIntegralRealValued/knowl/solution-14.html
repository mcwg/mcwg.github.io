Solution 5.1.1.

We have \(\vec{r}'(t)=-\sin{(t)} \vec{i} +\cos{(t)}\vec{j}\text{.}\) Therefore

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \int_{C} (x^2+y^2) ds \amp = \int_0^\pi \left((\cos t)^2+(\sin t)^2 \right) ||\vec{r}'(t)|| dt\\ \amp = \int_0^\pi \left((\cos t)^2+(\sin t)^2 \right)\sqrt{(-\sin t)^2+(-\cos t)^2}dt\\ \amp = \int_{0}^{\pi} 1~ dt\\ \amp = \pi \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
