Solution 3.3.1.

The line segment from \((,)\) to \((,-)\) can be parametrized by \[\vec{r}(t)=(t+)\vec{i}+(-t+)\vec{j},\quad 0\leq t\leq 1.\] Then we have \(\vec{r}'(t)=\vec{i} -\vec{j}\) and so

\begin{align*} \displaystyle\int_{C} (x-y) ds \amp = \int_0^{1} \left( (t+)-(-t+)\right) \sqrt{()^2+(-)^2} dt \\ \amp = \int_0^{1} (t+) \sqrt{} dt\\ \amp = \sqrt{} \left( \frac{t^2}{2} +t\right)\bigg|_{0}^{1}\\ \amp = \sqrt{}. \end{align*}
