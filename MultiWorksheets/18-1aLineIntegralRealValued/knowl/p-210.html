(b) We know \(\int_{C_2} f(x,y) ~ ds=0\text{.}\) Since \(C_1\) lies only on positive contours, the area under \(f(x,y)\) above \(C_1\) will be positive, so \(\int_{C_1} f(x,y)~ ds\gt 0\text{.}\) Similarly since \(C_3\) lies only on negative contours, the weighted area under \(f(x,y)\) above \(C_3\) will be negative (since it is below the \(xy\)-plane), so \(\int_{C_3} f(x,y)~ ds\lt 0\text{.}\) Thus

\begin{equation*} \int_{C_3} f(x,y) ~ds\lt \int_{C_2} f(x,y) ~ds\lt \int_{C_1} f(x,y) ~ds. \end{equation*}
