The curve \(y=x^2\) from \((0,0)\) to \(({5},{25})\) can be parametrized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(t)=t\vec{i}+t^2\vec{j},\quad 0\leq t\leq {5}. \end{equation*}

Then we have \(\vec{r}\,'(t)=\vec{i} +(2t)\vec{j}\) and so

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \int_{C} {4}x ~ ds \amp = \int_0^{{5}} \left({4}t \right) \sqrt{(1)^2+(2t)^2}~dt \\ \amp = \int_0^{{5}} {4}t \sqrt{1+4t^2}~dt\\ \amp = \left( {{\textstyle\frac{1}{3}}}\left(1+4t^2\right)^{\frac{3}{2}} \right)\bigg|_{0}^{{5}}\\ \amp = {{\textstyle\frac{1}{3}}}\left( \left({101}\right)^{\frac{3}{2}} -1\right).\\ \amp = {338.012} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
