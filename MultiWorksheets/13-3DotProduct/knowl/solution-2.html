Solution 4.2.1.

  1. Since \(\vec{v}\) and \(\vec{w}\) point more in the opposite direction than the same, this dot product should be negative.

  2. Since \(\vec{v}\) and \(\vec{w}\) point more in the opposite direction than the same, this dot product should be negative. Notice that this is the same as \(\vec{w} \cdot \vec{v}\text{.}\)

  3. Since \(\vec{v}\) and \(\vec{u}\) point more in the same direction than opposite, this dot product should be positive.

  4. Since \(\vec{v}\) and \(\vec{u}\) point more in the same direction than opposite, this dot product should be positive. Notice that this is the same as \(\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}\text{.}\)

  5. Since \(\vec{w}\) and \(\vec{u}\) are perpendicular, this dot product should be zero.

  6. Since \(\vec{w}\) and \(\vec{u}\) are perpendicular, this dot product should be zero. Notice that this is the same as \(\vec{u} \cdot \vec{w}\text{.}\)
