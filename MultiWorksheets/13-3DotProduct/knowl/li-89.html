Item 4.3.b.

False. If \(\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}=0\) and \(\vec{u} \cdot \vec{w}=0\text{,}\) then \(\vec{u}\) is perpendicular to both \(\vec{v}\) and \(\vec{w}\text{.}\) Since we are only in \({\mathbb R}^2\text{,}\) that can only happen if \(\vec{v}\) and \(\vec{w}\) either point in the same direction or opposite directions, and in either case, \(\vec{v} \cdot \vec{w}\neq 0\text{.}\)
