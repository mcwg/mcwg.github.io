Hint 4.1.1.

  1. Use the magnitude formula:

    \begin{equation*} ||\vec{v}||=\sqrt{v_{1}^2+v_{2}^2+...+v_{n}^2} \end{equation*}

  2. Use the magnitude formula:

    \begin{equation*} ||\vec{w}||=\sqrt{w_{1}^2+w_{2}^2+...+w_{n}^2} \end{equation*}

  3. Use the algebraic definition of the dot product:

    \begin{equation*} \vec{v} \cdot \vec{w} =v_{1}w_{1}+v_{2}w_{2}+\dots +v_{n}w_{n} \end{equation*}

  4. Starting with your answers to parts a, b, and c, use the geometric definition of the dot product:

    \begin{equation*} \vec{w} \cdot \vec{v} =||\vec{w} ||||\vec{v} ||\cos{\theta}, \end{equation*}

    where \(\theta\) is the angle between them.
