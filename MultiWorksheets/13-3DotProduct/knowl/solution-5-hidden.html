First we find a vector that is perpendicular to \(\vec{v}\text{,}\) and then we can normalize it to turn it into a unit vector. Let \(\vec{u}=a\vec{i}+b\vec{j}\text{.}\) In order to be perpendicular, we need the dot product to be \(0\text{,}\) or equivalently \(\vec{v} \cdot \vec{u} =({4})a+({3})b=0\text{.}\) One such solution is \(a={3}\) and \(b={-4}\text{,}\) so \(\vec{u} ={3\mathit{\vec i}-4\mathit{\vec j}}\text{.}\) Therefore, a unit vector perpendicular to \(\vec{v}\) will be \(\displaystyle\vec{w}=\frac{\vec{u}}{||\vec{u}||} ={\frac{3}{5}\mathit{\vec i}+\frac{-4}{5}\mathit{\vec j}}\text{.}\) A second possibility is the vector \(\displaystyle \vec{w}= -\frac{\vec{u}}{||\vec{u}||}\text{.}\)
