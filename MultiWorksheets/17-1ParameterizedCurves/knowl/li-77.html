Item.

For the equation \(\vec{r}(t) =t\vec{i} +t\vec{j} +t^3\vec{k}\) the \(\vec{i}\) and \(\vec{j}\) components parameterize a line, so the projection of this curve into the \(xy\)-plane is a line. The \(\vec{k}\) component gives the height of the curve \(C\) which increases at an increasing rate as \(t\) increases. It follows that \(\vec{r}(t) =t\vec{i} +t\vec{j} +t^3\vec{k}\) parameterizes a curve whose shadow is a line in the \(xy\)-plane and increases with a concave-up shape in the \(z\)-direction.
