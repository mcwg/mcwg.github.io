Item.

The equation \(\vec{r}(t)=(1-4t)\vec{i} +(2-3t)\vec{j}\) parameterizes a line that passes through the point \((1,2)\) in the direction of the vector \(\vec{v} =-4\vec{i} -3\vec{j}\text{.}\) Since \(\vec{r}(-1)=5\vec{i}+5\vec{j}=(5,5)\) and \(\vec{r}(2)=-7\vec{i} -4\vec{j}=(-7,-4)\text{,}\) this line segment begins in the first quadrant and ends in the third quadrant.
