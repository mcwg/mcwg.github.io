Since the circle occurs in the plane \(z={4}\text{,}\) the \(\vec{k}\) component of the parameterization must be \({4}\text{.}\) For a circle of radius \({2}\) whose center has been shifted from the origin by \({4}\) in each direction, we use \({2}\cos{(t)}+{4}\) and \({2}\sin{(t)}+{4}\) as component functions, and since it occurs in the plane \(z={4}\text{,}\) they are component functions of the \(\vec{i}\) and \(\vec{j}\) components. Thus a possible parameterization is

\begin{equation*} {\left(4+2\cos\!\left(t\right)\right)\mathit{\vec i}+\left(4+2\sin\!\left(t\right)\right)\mathit{\vec j}+4\mathit{\vec k}} \end{equation*}
