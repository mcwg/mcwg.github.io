For the equation \(\vec{r}(t) =t\cos{(t)}\vec{i} +t\sin{(t)}\vec{j} +t\vec{k}\) we first consider the \(\vec{i}\) and \(\vec{j}\) components. The \(\cos{(t)}\) and \(\sin{(t)}\) in these components imply rotational motion and the multiple of \(t\) implies increased radius of rotation as \(t\) increases. It follows that the \(\vec{i}\) and \(\vec{j}\) components parameterize a spiral in the \(xy\)-plane spiraling out from the origin in a counterclockwise direction, so the projection of this curve into the \(xy\)-plane is this spiral. The \(\vec{k}\) component gives the height of the curve \(C\) which increases as \(t\) increases. It follows that \(\vec{r}(t) =t\cos{(t)}\vec{i} +t\sin{(t)}\vec{j} +t\vec{k}\) parameterizes a spiral around the \(z\)-axis, whose radius increases with height, similar to the shape of a tornado.
