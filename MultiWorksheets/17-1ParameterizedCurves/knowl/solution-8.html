The correct answers are B, E, A, D, F, C. Explanations are below.

  • The equation \(\vec{r}(t)=t\vec{i} +\sin{(t)}\vec{j}\) parameterizes the graph of the function \(y=\sin{(x)}\text{.}\) Since \(0\leq t\leq \pi\text{,}\) it is the portion of the graph with \(0\leq x\leq \pi\text{.}\)

  • The equation \(\vec{r}(t)=\sin{(t)}\vec{i} +\cos{(t)}\vec{j}\) parameterizes a circle of radius \(1\) centered at the origin. Since the values at the endpoints are \(\vec{r}(0)=\vec{j}=(0,1)\) and \(\vec{r}(\pi)=-\vec{j}=(0,-1)\text{,}\) and the value at \(t=\pi/2\) is \(\vec i\text{,}\) this is the half of the circle to the right of the \(y\)-axis.

  • The equation \(\vec{r}(t)=\cos{(t)} \vec{i} +t\vec{j}\) parameterizes the graph of the function \(x=\cos{(y)}\text{.}\) Since \(0\leq t\leq \pi\text{,}\) it is the portion of the graph with \(0\leq y\leq \pi\text{.}\)

  • The equation \(\vec{r}(t)=(1-4t)\vec{i} +(2-3t)\vec{j}\) parameterizes a line that passes through the point \((1,2)\) in the direction of the vector \(\vec{v} =-4\vec{i} -3\vec{j}\text{.}\) Since \(\vec{r}(1)=-3\vec{i}-\vec{j}=(-3,-1)\) and \(\vec{r}(3)=-11\vec{i}-7\vec{j}=(-11,-7)\text{,}\) this line segment is entirely in the third quadrant.

  • The equation \(\vec{r}(t)=(1-4t)\vec{i} +(2-3t)\vec{j}\) parameterizes a line that passes through the point \((1,2)\) in the direction of the vector \(\vec{v} =-4\vec{i} -3\vec{j}\text{.}\) Since \(\vec{r}(-1)=5\vec{i}+5\vec{j}=(5,5)\) and \(\vec{r}(2)=-7\vec{i} -4\vec{j}=(-7,-4)\text{,}\) this line segment begins in the first quadrant and ends in the third quadrant.

  • The equation \(\vec{r}(t)=\cos{(t)}\vec{i} +\sin{(t)}\vec{j}\) parameterizes a circle of radius \(1\) centered at the origin and oriented counter-clockwise. Since \(\vec{r}(0)=\vec{i}=(1,0)\) and \(\vec{r}(\pi)=-\vec{i}=(-1,0)\) this is the half of the circle above the \(x\)-axis.
