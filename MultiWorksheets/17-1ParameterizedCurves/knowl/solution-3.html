Solution 4.3.1.

Recall that a circle with radius \(R\) centered at the point \((a,b)\) and oriented counter-clockwise is parameterized by \(\vec{r}(t) =(R\cos (t) +a)\vec{i} +(R\sin (t) +b)\vec{j}\text{.}\) Since \(({2},{5})\) and \(({4},{7})\) are on the same diameter, the point \((a,b)\) is their midpoint, which is \(({3},{6})\text{.}\) To calculate the radius, we use the distance formula from one of these points to the center, getting \(R=\sqrt{({2}-({3}))^2+({5}-({6}))^2}={\sqrt{2}}\text{.}\) Thus we get \(\vec{r}(t)=({3}+{\sqrt{2}} \cos{(t)})\vec{i} +({6}+{\sqrt{2}}\sin{(t)})\vec{j}\text{.}\)
