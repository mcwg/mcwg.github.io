The equation \(\vec{r}(t)=\sin{(t)}\vec{i} +\cos{(t)}\vec{j}\) parameterizes a circle of radius \(1\) centered at the origin. Since the values at the endpoints are \(\vec{r}(0)=\vec{j}=(0,1)\) and \(\vec{r}(\pi)=-\vec{j}=(0,-1)\text{,}\) and the value at \(t=\pi/2\) is \(\vec i\text{,}\) this is the half of the circle to the right of the \(y\)-axis.
