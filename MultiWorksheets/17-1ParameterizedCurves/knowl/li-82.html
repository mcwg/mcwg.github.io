Item.

For the equation \(\vec{r}(t)=\vec{i} +\cos{(t)}\vec{j} +\sin{(t)}\vec{k}\) with \(0\leq t\leq \pi\text{,}\) the \(\vec{j}\) and \(\vec{k}\) components parameterize a half-circle (since \(0\leq t\leq \pi\)) centered at the origin in the \(yz\)-plane, so the projection of this curve into the \(yz\)-plane is a half-circle. Since the \(\vec{i}\) component is constant, the \(x\)-value of this curve never changes. It follows that \(\vec{r}(t)=\vec{i} +\cos{(t)}\vec{j} +\sin{(t)}\vec{k}\) parameterizes a half-circle parallel to the \(yz\)-plane at \(x=1\text{.}\)
