Recall that a line line passing through point \(P\) in the direction of the vector \(\vec{v}\) is parameterized by \(\vec{r} (t) =\vec{P} +\vec{v} t\text{.}\) In this case, a vector \(\vec{v}\) that points in the direction of the line is the vector pointing between the two points, or \(\vec{v} =({2}-{0})\vec{i} +({3}-{3})\vec{j} +({4}-{6})\vec{k} ={2}\vec{i}{-2}\vec{k}\text{.}\) Then, taking the point \(P=({2},{3},{4})\text{,}\) so that \(\vec P ={2}\vec{i} +{3}\vec{j} +{4}\vec{k}\) and combining like components, this line is parameterized by

\begin{equation*} {2}\vec{i} +{3}\vec{j} +{4}\vec{k}+({2}\vec{i} {-2}\vec{k})t=({2}+{2}t)\vec{i} +{3}\vec{j}+({4}{-2}t)\vec{k}. \end{equation*}
