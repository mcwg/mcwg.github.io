The equation \(\vec{r}(t)=\cos{(t)}\vec{i} +\sin{(t)}\vec{j}\) parameterizes a circle of radius \(1\) centered at the origin and oriented counter-clockwise. Since \(\vec{r}(0)=\vec{i}=(1,0)\) and \(\vec{r}(\pi)=-\vec{i}=(-1,0)\) this is the half of the circle above the \(x\)-axis.
