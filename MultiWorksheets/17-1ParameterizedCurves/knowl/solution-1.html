Solution 4.1.1.

Recall that a line passing through point \(P\) in the direction of the vector \(\vec{v}\) is parameterized by \(\vec{r} (t) =\vec{P} +\vec{v} t\text{.}\) In this case, we have \(\vec{v}={5}\vec{i}+{5}\vec{j}{-4}\vec{k}\) and \(\vec{P}=\langle{2},{5},{2}\rangle={2}\vec{i} +{5}\vec{j} +{2}\vec{k}\text{.}\) Thus, combining like components, this line is parameterized by

\begin{equation*} {2}\vec{i} +{5}\vec{j} +{2}\vec{k}+({5}\vec{i} +{5}\vec{j} {-4}\vec{k})t=({2}+{5}t)\vec{i} +({5}+{5}t)\vec{j}+({2}{-4}t)\vec{k}. \end{equation*}
