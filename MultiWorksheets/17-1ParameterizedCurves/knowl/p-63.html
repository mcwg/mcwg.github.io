Since the circle occurs in the \(yz\)-plane where \(x=0\text{,}\) the \(\vec{i}\) component of the parameterization must be \(0\text{.}\) For a circle of radius \({4}\) whose center has not been shifted from the origin we use \({4}\cos{(t)}\) and \({4}\sin{(t)}\) as component functions, and since it occurs in the \(yz\)-plane, they are component functions of the \(\vec{j}\) and \(\vec{k}\) components. Thus a possible parameterization is \(\vec{r}(t)={4}\cos{(t)}\vec{j} +{4}\sin{(t)}\vec{k}\text{.}\)
