The equation \(\vec{r}(t)=(1-4t)\vec{i} +(2-3t)\vec{j}\) parameterizes a line that passes through the point \((1,2)\) in the direction of the vector \(\vec{v} =-4\vec{i} -3\vec{j}\text{.}\) Since \(\vec{r}(1)=-3\vec{i}-\vec{j}=(-3,-1)\) and \(\vec{r}(3)=-11\vec{i}-7\vec{j}=(-11,-7)\text{,}\) this line segment is entirely in the third quadrant.
