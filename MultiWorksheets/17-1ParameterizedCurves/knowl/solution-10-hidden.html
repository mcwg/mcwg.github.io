Recall that a circle with radius \(R\) centered at the point \((a,b)\) and oriented counter-clockwise is parameterized by \(\vec{r}(t) =(R\cos (t) +a)\vec{i} +(R\sin (t) +b)\vec{j}\text{.}\) To modify the parameterization to give an ellipse, we replace the radius \(R\) in the \(\vec{i}\)-component with \({5}\text{,}\) the radius in the \(x\)-direction, and we replace the radius \(R\) in the \(\vec{j}\)-component with \({3}\text{,}\) the radius in the \(y\)-direction. This gives \(\vec{r}(t)=({-5}+{5}\cos{(t)}))\vec{i} +({4}+{3}\sin{(t)})\vec{j}\text{.}\) To orientate the curve clockwise, we replace \(t\) with \(-t\) giving

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \vec{r}(t) \amp =({-5}+{5}\cos{(-t)})\vec{i} +({4}+{3}\sin{(-t)})\vec{j} \\ \amp = ({-5}+{5}\cos{(t)})\vec{i} +({4}-{3}\sin{(t)})\vec{j}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
