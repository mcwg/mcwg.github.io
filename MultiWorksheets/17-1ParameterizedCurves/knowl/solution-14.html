Solution 6.5.1.

Recall that a vector equation for a plane is \(\vec{p} (s,t)=\vec{P} +s \vec{v} +t\vec{w}\) where \(P\) is a point in the plane and \(\vec{v}\) and \(\vec{w}\) are non-parallel, non-zero vectors in the plane. We are already given three points to choose from and for vectors \(\vec{v}\) and \(\vec{w}\text{,}\) we can take vectors between two different points on the plane. Specifically, we can choose \(\vec{v} =\vec{PQ} ={-3\mathit{\vec i}+5\mathit{\vec j}+3\mathit{\vec k}}\) and \(\vec{w} =\vec{PR} ={-\mathit{\vec j}-5\mathit{\vec k}}\text{.}\) Then, choosing \(\vec{P} ={\mathit{\vec i}-3\mathit{\vec j}+\mathit{\vec k}}\) and combining like components, a vector equation for the plane is

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \vec{p} (s,t) \amp =({\mathit{\vec i}-3\mathit{\vec j}+\mathit{\vec k}}) +s ({-3\mathit{\vec i}+5\mathit{\vec j}+3\mathit{\vec k}}) +t({-\mathit{\vec j}-5\mathit{\vec k}}) \\ \amp ={\left(1-3s\right)\mathit{\vec i}+\left(5s-3-t\right)\mathit{\vec j}+\left(1+3s-5t\right)\mathit{\vec k}} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
