For the equation \(\vec{r}(t)=\cos{(t)}\vec{i} +\sin{(t)}\vec{j} +t\vec{k}\text{,}\) the \(\vec{i}\) and \(\vec{j}\) components parameterize a circle in the \(xy\)-plane centered at the origin, so the projection of this curve into the \(xy\)-plane is a circle. The \(\vec{k}\) component gives the height of the curve \(C\) which increases as \(t\) increases. It follows that \(\vec{r}(t)=\cos{(t)}\vec{i} +\sin{(t)}\vec{j} +t\vec{k}\) parameterizes a helix around the \(z\)-axis.
