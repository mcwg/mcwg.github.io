Recall that a line passing through point \(P\) in the direction of the vector \(\vec{v}\) is parameterized by \(\vec{r} (t) =\vec{P} +\vec{v} t\text{.}\) In this case, a vector \(\vec{v}\) that points in the direction of the line segment is the vector pointing from \(({-5},{3})\) to \(({3},{-5})\text{,}\) so \(\vec{v} =({3}-({-5}))\vec{i} +({-5}-{3})\vec{j} ={8}\vec{i} {-8}\vec{j}\text{.}\) Then, taking the initial point \(P=({-5},{3})={-5}\vec{i} +{3}\vec{j}\) and combining like components, this line is parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(t)={-5}\vec{i} +{3}\vec{j}+({8}\vec{i} {-8}\vec{j})t=({-5}+{8}t)\vec{i} +({3}{-8}t)\vec{j}. \end{equation*}

To restrict to the line segment, we must have \(0\leq t\leq 1\) since \(\vec{r}(0)={-5}\vec{i} +{3}\vec{j}\text{,}\) the first point, and \(\vec{r}(1)={3}\vec{i} {-5}\vec{j}\text{,}\) the second point.
