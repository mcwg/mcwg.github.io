Recall that a circle with radius \(R\) centered at the point \((a,b)\) and oriented counter-clockwise is parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(t) =(R\cos (t) +a)\vec{i} +(R\sin (t) +b)\vec{j}\text{.} \end{equation*}

  1. We see from the parameterization that the center is \(({3},{-2})\)

  2. We see from the parameterization that the radius is \({5}\text{.}\)

  3. Since the curve is oriented counterclockwise, to restrict to the upper half of the circle we need the initial bound on \(t\) to give the right-most point \(({8},{-2})\) on the circle. One possibility is \(t=0\text{.}\) Next, the upper bound on \(t\) must give the left-most point \(({-2},{-2})\text{,}\) without traversing the circle more than once. The only possibility is \(t=\pi\text{,}\) and thus we restrict to \(0\leq t\leq \pi\text{.}\)
