Item 4.1.d.

Since \(f_x={0}\text{,}\) we have \(f_{xx}={0}\) and \(f_{xy}={0}\text{.}\) Next, since \(f_y={3\cos\!\left(y\right)e^{y}-3\sin\!\left(y\right)e^{y}}\text{,}\) we have \(f_{yy}={3\cos\!\left(y\right)e^{y}-3\sin\!\left(y\right)e^{y}-\left(3\cos\!\left(y\right)e^{y}+3\sin\!\left(y\right)e^{y}\right)}\) and \(f_{yx}={0}\text{.}\) Observe that \(f_{xy}={0}=f_{yx}\text{.}\)
