Solution 4.5.1.

We consider each derivative individually:

  1. \(f_{x}(2,-1)\) is zero since the contour values does not change in the \(x\)-direction.

  2. \(f_{y}(2,-1)\) is positive since the contour values go up from \((2,-1)\) in the \(y\)-direction.

  3. \(f_{xx}(2,-1)\) is zero since the slope \(f_x\) does not change in the \(x\)-direction.

  4. \(f_{yy}(2,-1)\) is negative since \(f_y\) is positive and the contours are getting further apart in the \(y\)-direction (so the slope is getting less steep).

  5. \(f_{xy}(2,-1)\) is positive since \(f_x\) is \(0\) at \((2,-1)\text{,}\) but as we move in the \(y\)-direction \(f_x\) becomes positive.
