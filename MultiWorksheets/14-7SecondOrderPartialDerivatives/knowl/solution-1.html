Solution 4.1.1.

  1. Since \(f_x={2x}\text{,}\) we have \(f_{xx}={2}\) and \(f_{xy}=0\text{.}\) Next, since \(f_y={5y^{4}}\text{,}\) we have \(f_{yy}={20x^{3}}\) and \(f_{yx}=0\text{.}\) Observe that \(f_{xy}=0=f_{yx}\text{.}\)

  2. Since \(f_x={2xy^{5}}\text{,}\) we have \(f_{xx}={2y^{5}}\) and \(f_{xy}={10xy^{4}}\text{.}\) Next, since \(f_y={5x^{2}y^{4}}\text{,}\) we have \(f_{yy}={20x^{2}y^{3}}\) and \(f_{yx}={10xy^{4}}\text{.}\) Observe that \(f_{xy}={10xy^{4}}=f_{yx}\text{.}\)
