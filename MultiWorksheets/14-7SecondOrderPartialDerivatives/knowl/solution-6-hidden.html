1. If the point \((a,b)\) is a maximum of \(f(x,y)\text{,}\) then it is a maximum in the \(x\)- and \(y\)-directions. This means that \(f_{x}(a,b)=f_{y}(a,b)=0\text{.}\) Regarding \(f_{xx}(a,b)\) and \(f_{yy}(a,b)\text{,}\) note that since the graph has to be concave down in both the \(x\)- and \(y\)-directions, \(f_{xx}(a,b)\) and \(f_{yy}(a,b)\) are either zero or negative (they can be zero in the same way that \(g(t)=-t^4\) has a maximum at \(t=0\) but \(g''(0)=0\)).

  2. Similarly, since \((a,b)\) is a minimum in the \(x\) and \(y\)-directions, then \(f_{x}(a,b)=f_{y}(a,b)=0\text{,}\) and \(f_{xx}(a,b)\) and \(f_{yy}(a,b)\) are zero or positive.
