Item 4.2.a.

We consider each derivative individually:

  1. \(f_{x}(-1,1)\) is negative since the contour values go down from \((-1,1)\) in the \(x\)-direction.

  2. \(f_{y}(-1,1)\) is negative since the contour values go down from \((-1,1)\) in the \(y\)-direction.

  3. \(f_{xx}(-1,1)\) is positive since \(f_x\) is negative and the contours are getting further apart in the \(x\)-direction (so the slope is getting “less negative”).

  4. \(f_{yy}(-1,1)\) is negative since \(f_y\) is negative and the contours are getting closer together in the \(y\)-direction (so the slope is getting “more negative”).

  5. \(f_{xy}(-1,1)\) is negative since \(f_x\) is negative and the contours in the \(x\)-direction get closer together as we move in the \(y\)-direction (so the slope is getting “more negative”).
