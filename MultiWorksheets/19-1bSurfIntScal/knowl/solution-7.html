Solution 5.5.1.

If we follow the definition with \(f(x,y,z)=1\text{,}\) we see that,

\begin{equation*} \int \int_{S} 1 \,dS =\lim_{n,m\rightarrow \infty} \sum_{j=1}^{m} \sum_{i=1}^{n} \, ||(\vec{r}_{u} \times \vec{r}_{v})(u_{i}^* , v_{j}^*)||\,\Delta u \Delta v, \end{equation*}

where the sum is over the division of \(D\) into small rectangles of lengths \(\Delta u\) in the \(u\)-direction and \(\Delta v\) in the \(v\)-direction and area \(\Delta u \Delta v\text{.}\)

The term

\begin{equation*} ||(\vec{r}_{u} \times \vec{r}_{v}) (u_{i}^* , v_{j}^*)|| \Delta u \Delta v, \end{equation*}

in the sum is an approximation for the area of a patch of \(S\) which is the image of the small rectangle in \(D\text{,}\) since the vectors \(\vec{r}_{u} (u_{i}^* , v_{j}^*) \Delta u\) and \(\vec{r}_{v} (u_{i}^* , v_{j}^*) \Delta v\) approximate the length of the sides in the \(u\)-direction and \(v\)-direction.

Thus,

\begin{equation*} \int \int_{S} 1 \,dS=\int \int_{D} ||\vec{r}_{u} \times \vec{r}_{v}(u,v)||\,du \,dv \end{equation*}

is the surface area of \(S\text{.}\)
