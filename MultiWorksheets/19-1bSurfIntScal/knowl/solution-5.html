Solution 5.3.1.

Note that by definition

\begin{equation*} \int \int_{S} f(x,y,z)\,dS =\int \int_{D} f(\vec{r}(u,v)) \,||\vec{N}|| \,du\,dv, \end{equation*}

where \(\vec{r}(u,v)\) is a parameterization of \(S\) and \(D\) is the corresponding region in the \(uv\)-plane. In particular, the sign of \(f\) on the points of the surface \(S\) determines the sign of the integrand \(f(\vec{r}(u,v)) \,||\vec{N}||\text{.}\) In this case \(f(x,y)={y}\text{.}\)

We now consider the integrals over \(S_1\) and \(S_2\text{:}\)

  1. \({y}\) is a positive function on all of \(S_1\text{,}\) so \(\displaystyle \int\int_{S_1}{y} \,dS\) is positive.

  2. \({y}\) is a negative function on all of \(S_2\text{,}\) so \(\displaystyle \int\int_{S_2}{y} \,dS\) is negative.
