The sphere \(x^2+y^2+z^2={7}\) has cross-sections which consist of concentric circles centered on the \(z\)-axis whose radii change at different heights. Specifically, for a given \(z\) since we can rewrite \(x^2+y^2+z^2={7}\) as \(x^2+y^2=({7} -z^2)=r^2\text{,}\) the radius \(r\) of the cross-section at height \(z\) is \(r=\sqrt{{7} -z^2}\text{.}\) Recall that a surface \(S\) with cross-sections which consist of concentric circles (or parts of concentric circles) centered on the \(z\)-axis whose radii are a function of height \(f(z)\text{,}\) can be parameterized by letting \(z=v\text{,}\) \(x=f(v)\cos{(u)}\text{,}\) and \(y=f(v)\sin{(u)}\text{.}\)

Thus, the sphere can be parameterized by letting \(z=v\text{,}\) \(x=\sqrt{{7} -v^2}\cos{(u)}\text{,}\) and \(y=\sqrt{{7} -v^2}\sin{(u)}\text{,}\) or

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(u,v) =\sqrt{{7} -v^2}\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +\sqrt{{7} -v^2}\sin{(u)} \vec{j} +v\vec{k} \text{.} \end{equation*}
