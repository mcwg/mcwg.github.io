Item 3.

Recall that a line integral of a scalar function \(f\) over a curve \(C\) parameterized by \(\vec{r}(t)\) for \(a\leq t\leq b\) is calculated using the formula

\begin{equation*} \int_{C} fds=\int_{a}^{b} f(\vec{r}(t)) \, ||\vec{r}'(t)||\,dt. \end{equation*}

Describe the differences and similarities to the surface integral \(\int \int_{S} f(x,y,z)\,dS\text{.}\) That is, describe how the formulas differ or share similarities and how the process of calculation differs or share similarities.
