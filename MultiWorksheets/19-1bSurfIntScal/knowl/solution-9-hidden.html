First, \(S\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(u,v)={9}\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +{9}\sin{(u)} \vec{j} +v\vec{k}\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(0\leq v\leq {9}\) and \(0\leq u\leq \pi\text{.}\)

The normal vector is \(\vec{N} ={9}\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +{9}\sin{(u)} \vec{j}\text{,}\) so \(||N||={9}\text{.}\) Thus, we have

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \int\int_{S} x \,dS \amp =\int_{0}^{{9}} \int_{0}^{\pi} {9}{\cos\!\left(u\right)} \cdot {9}\,du\,dv\\ \amp ={81} \int_{0}^{{9}} \int_{0}^{\pi} {\cos\!\left(u\right)} \,du\,dv\\ \amp ={81}\cdot {9\sin\!\left(u\right)}\Big|_{0}^{\pi}\\ \amp =0. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
