First, \(S\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(u,v)=\cos{(u)} \vec{i} +v \vec{j} +\sin{(u)}\vec{k}\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(0\leq v\leq {5}\) and \(0\leq u\leq \pi\text{.}\)

The normal vector is \(\vec{N} =-\cos{(u)} \vec{i} -\sin{(u)}\vec{k}\text{,}\) so \(||N||=1\text{.}\) Thus, we have

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \int \int_{S} z\,dS \amp =\int_{0}^{{5}} \int_{0}^{\pi} {\sin\!\left(u\right)} \,du\,dv\\ \amp ={5} \left( -\cos(u) \right)\Big|_{0}^{\pi} \\ \amp ={5} \left( -\cos(\pi) -(-\cos(0)) \right)\\ \amp ={5}\cdot (1-(-1))\\ \amp ={10}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
