First, \(S\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(u,v)=\sqrt{{49}-v^2} \cos{(u)} \vec{i} +\sqrt{{49}-v^2} \sin{(u)} \vec{j} +v\vec{k} \end{equation*}

where \(0\leq v\leq {7}\) and \(0\leq u\leq 2\pi\text{.}\)

The normal vector is \(\vec{N} =\sqrt{{49}-v^2} \cos{(u)} \vec{i} +\sqrt{{49}-v^2} \sin{(u)} \vec{j} +v\vec{k}\text{,}\) so \(||N||={7}\text{.}\) Thus, we have

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \int\int_{S} ({3}x^2+{3}y^2+{3}z^2)dS \amp =\int_{0}^{{7}} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \left[ {3}\left(\sqrt{{49}-v^2} \cos{(u)}\right)^2\right.\\ \amp\qquad \left.+{3}\left(\sqrt{{49}-v^2} \sin{(u)}\right)^2 +{3}v^2\right]({7})\,du\,dv\\ \amp ={21} \int_{0}^{{7}} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \left[ ({49}-v^2) \cos^2{(u)}\right.\\ \amp\qquad\left.+({49}-v^2) \sin^2{(u)}+v^2\right]\,du\,dv\\ \amp ={21} \int_{0}^{{7}} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \left( {49}-v^2+v^2\right)\,du\,dv\\ \amp ={21} \int_{0}^{{7}} \int_{0}^{2\pi} {49}\,du\,dv\\ \amp ={21}\cdot {7}\cdot 2\pi \cdot {49}\\ \amp = {14406}\pi. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
