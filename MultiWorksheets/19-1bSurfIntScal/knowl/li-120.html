Item 1.

Answer the following as True or False with a brief reason.

  1. If the surface \(S\) is below the \(xy\)-plane, then the integral \(\int \int_{S} f(x,y,z)\,dS\) is negative.
  2. If \(S\) is the full sphere \(x^2+y^2+z^2=1\) and \(D\) the upper half portion of that sphere, then \(\int \int_{S} f(x,y,z)\,dS=2\int \int_{D} f(x,y,z)\,dS\text{.}\)
  3. If \(f(x,y,z)\geq g(x,y,z)\) for all \((x,y,z)\text{,}\) then \(\int \int_{S} f(x,y,z)\,dS\geq \int \int_{S} g(x,y,z)\,dS\) for any surface \(S\text{.}\)
  4. The surface integral \(\int \int_{S} f(x,y,z)\,dS\) is independent of orientation of the surface \(S\text{.}\)
