First, \(S\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(u,v)=\sqrt{{25}-v^2} \cos{(u)} \vec{i} +\sqrt{{25}-v^2} \sin{(u)} \vec{j} +v\vec{k} \end{equation*}

where \(0\leq v\leq {5}\) and \(0\leq u\leq 2\pi\text{.}\)

The normal vector is

\begin{equation*} \vec{N} =\sqrt{{25}-v^2} \cos{(u)} \vec{i} +\sqrt{{25}-v^2} \sin{(u)} \vec{j} +v\vec{k} \end{equation*}

and so \(||N||={5}\text{.}\) Thus we have

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int_{S} ({17}-x^2-y^2-z^2)\,dS \amp =\int_{0}^{{5}} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \left[ {17}-\left(\sqrt{{25}-v^2} \cos{(u)}\right)^2 \right.\\ \amp \quad \left. -\left(\sqrt{{25}-v^2} \sin{(u)}\right)^2 -v^2\right]\cdot {17}\,du\,dv\\ \amp ={5} \int_{0}^{{5}} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \left( {17}-({25}-v^2)-v^2\right)\,du\,dv\\ \amp ={5} \int_{0}^{{5}} \int_{0}^{2\pi} {-8}\,du\,dv\\ \amp ={5}\cdot {5}\cdot 2\pi \cdot {-8}\\ \amp = {-400}\pi. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
