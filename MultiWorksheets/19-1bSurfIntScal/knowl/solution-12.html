Solution 6.5.1.

First, \(S\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(u,v)=u \vec{i} +v \vec{j} +(u^2+v^2)\vec{k}. \end{equation*}

Since we want \(z\leq {16}\text{,}\) the region \(D\) mut be the disc \(z=u^2+v^2\leq {16}\text{.}\) The normal vector is \(\vec{N} =-2u \vec{i} -2v \vec{j} +\vec{k}\text{,}\) so \(||N||=\sqrt{1+4u^2+4v^2}\text{.}\) Therefore, we have

\begin{equation*} \int\int_{S} {9}dS =\int \int_D {9}\sqrt{1+4u^2+4v^2}dudv. \end{equation*}

Since we are integrating over a disc, we use a polar change of coordinates with bounds \(0\leq r\leq {4}\) and \(0\leq \theta \leq 2\pi\) giving:

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \int\int_{S} {9}\,dS \amp =\int \int_D {9}\sqrt{1+4u^2+4v^2}\,du\,dv\\ \amp ={9} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{{4}} \sqrt{\left(1+4r^2\cos^2{(\theta )}+4r^2\sin^2{(\theta )}\right)} \,rdr\,d\theta\\ \amp ={9} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{{4}} \left(1+4r^2\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} \,rdr\,d\theta\\ \amp ={9} \cdot 2\pi \left(1+4r^2\right)^{\frac{3}{2}} \cdot \frac{2}{3} \cdot \frac{1}{8} \bigg|_{r=0}^{r={4}}\\ \amp ={\frac{3\pi }{2}} \left( {65}^{\frac{3}{2}} -1\right). \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
