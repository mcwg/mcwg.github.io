First, \(S\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(u,v)=\sqrt{-v^2} \cos{(u)} \vec{i} +\sqrt{-v^2} \sin{(u)} \vec{j} +v\vec{k} \end{equation*}

where \(0\leq v\leq \) and \(0\leq u\leq 2\pi\text{.}\)

The normal vector is

\begin{equation*} \vec{N} =\sqrt{-v^2} \cos{(u)} \vec{i} +\sqrt{-v^2} \sin{(u)} \vec{j} +v\vec{k} \end{equation*}

and so \(||N||=\text{.}\) Thus we have

\begin{align*} \int_{S} (-x^2-y^2-z^2)\,dS \amp =\int_{0}^{} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \left[ -\left(\sqrt{-v^2} \cos{(u)}\right)^2 \right.\\ \amp \quad \left. -\left(\sqrt{-v^2} \sin{(u)}\right)^2 -v^2\right]\cdot \,du\,dv\\ \amp = \int_{0}^{} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \left( -(-v^2)-v^2\right)\,du\,dv\\ \amp = \int_{0}^{} \int_{0}^{2\pi} \,du\,dv\\ \amp =\cdot \cdot 2\pi \cdot \\ \amp = \pi. \end{align*}
