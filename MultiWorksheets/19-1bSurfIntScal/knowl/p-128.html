We now consider the integrals over \(S_1\) and \(S_2\text{:}\)

  1. \(f(x,y)={40}+(x^2+y^2+z^2)\) is a positive function on all of \(S\text{,}\) so \(\displaystyle \int\int_{S}{40}+(x^2+y^2+z^2) \,dS\) is positive.

  2. \(f(x,y)={40}-(x^2+y^2+z^2)\) is exactly equal to zero on all of \(S\text{,}\) so \(\displaystyle \int\int_{S}{40}-(x^2+y^2+z^2) \,dS\) is zero.

  3. \(f(x,y)={40}-({2}x^2+{2}y^2+{2}z^2)\) is negative on all of \(S\text{,}\) so \(\displaystyle \int\int_{S}{40}-({2}x^2+{2}y^2+{2}z^2) \,dS\) is negative.
