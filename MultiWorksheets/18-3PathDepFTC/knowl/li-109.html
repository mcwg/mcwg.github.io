Item 2.

Are the following statements true or false? Give reasons for your answers.

  1. Any vector field whose component functions are constants is conservative.

  2. If \(\vec{F}\) is conservative and \(C\) is a curve parameterized by \(\vec{r}(t)\) with \(a\leq t\leq b\text{,}\) then \(\int_{C} \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r} =\vec{r}(b)-\vec{r}(a)\text{.}\)

  3. If \(\vec{F}\) is conservative and \(C\) is a closed curve, then \(\int_{C} \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r} =0\text{.}\)

  4. If \(\vec{F}\) is conservative and \(C\) is not closed, then \(\int_{C} \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r} \neq 0\text{.}\)

  5. If \(\vec{F}\) is not conservative and \(C_1\) and \(C_2\) are paths between the same two points, then \(\int_{C_1} \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r} \neq \int_{C_2} \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r}\text{.}\)
