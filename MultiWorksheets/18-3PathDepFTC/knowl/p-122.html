Based on the figure, if we think of placing a little flywheel at some point \(P\) on the \(x\)-axis, the vectors to the left and right cancel each others attempts to rotate the flywheel while vectors above and below \(P\) definitely make the flywheel rotate clockwise. This means that \(\vec{F}\) induces a clockwise rotation around any point on the \(x\)-axis, so the scalar curl is negative at any of these points and \(\vec{F}\) is definitely not conservative.
