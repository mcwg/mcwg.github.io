Solution 5.1.1.

A region \(U\) is simply connected if every simple closed curve completely contained in \(C\) can be shrunk to a point in \(U\) without breaking and without leaving \(U\text{.}\) For a region in \({\mathbb R}^2\text{,}\) this is equivalent to there being no holes contained within the region.

  1. This region is not simply connected as any loop around the middle circle could never be pulled to a point without leaving the region.

  2. This region is simply connected as there are no holes.

  3. This region is not simply connected as any loop around the hole in the center of the region could never be pulled to a point without leaving the region.

  4. This region is simply connected since even though there is a hole on the edge of the region; this would not prevent a closed curve from being pulled to a point within the region since no closed curve could lie entirely within this region, and encircle this hole at the same time.
