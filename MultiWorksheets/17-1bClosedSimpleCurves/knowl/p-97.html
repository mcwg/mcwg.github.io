First we have \(\vec{r}(0)={5}\vec{i} +2\vec{j}\) and \(\vec{r}(1 )={5}\vec{i} +2\vec{j}\text{,}\) so \(C\) is closed since the endpoints coincide. By the same reasoning it is not simple since it intersects itself (at the endpoints). Finally, observe that \(\vec{r}(t)\) parameterizes an ellipse and since \(0\leq t\leq 1\text{,}\) the circle is traversed exactly once. In particular, there are no intersection points except the endpoints, so it follows that \(C\) is simple-closed.
