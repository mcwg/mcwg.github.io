Solution 5.2.1.

First we have \(\vec{r}(0)={2}\vec{j}\) and \(\vec{r}(4\pi )={2}\vec{j} +4\pi (4\pi -2\pi)\vec{k} ={2}\vec{j} +8\pi^2\vec{k}\text{.}\) In particular, the endpoints of \(C\) do not match and hence \(C\) is neither closed nor simple-closed. Next observe that \(\vec{r}(0)={2}\vec{j}=\vec{r}(2\pi)\text{,}\) so \(C\) intersects itself at the point \((0,{2},0)\) and so is not simple.
