Remember that if \(\vec{r}(t)\text{,}\) for \(a\leq t\leq b\text{,}\) is a smooth parameterization of an oriented curve \(C\) and \(\vec{F}\) is a vector field which is continuous on \(C\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} \int_C \vec{F}\cdot d\vec{r}=\int_a^b \vec{F}(\vec{r}(t))\cdot \vec{r}'(t) dt. \end{equation*}

The curve \(y=e^x\) from \((0,1)\) to \(({2},e^{2})\) can be parameterized by \(t\vec{i}+(e^{t})\vec{j}\) with \(0\leq t\leq {2}\text{.}\) To find a parameterization from \(({2},e^{2})\) to \((0,1)\) we reverse the orientation by replacing \(t\) by \(-t\text{.}\) Thus, the curve \(y=e^x\) from \(({2},e^{2})\) to \((0,1)\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(t)=-t\vec{i}+(e^{-t})\vec{j},\quad -{2}\leq t\leq 0, \end{equation*}

and we have

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}'(t)=-\vec{i} -e^{-t}\vec{j}. \end{equation*}

Thus

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int_{C} \left( x^2\vec{i} - y\vec{j} \right) \cdot d\vec{r} \amp = \int_{-{2}}^{0} \left( (-t)^2\vec{i}-(e^{-t})\vec{j}\right) \cdot \left(-\vec{i}-e^{-t}\vec{j} \right)dt\\ \amp = \int_{-{2}}^{0} -t^2+e^{-2t} dt \\ \amp = \left( -\frac{t^3}{3}-\frac{e^{-2t}}{2}\right)\bigg|_{-{2}}^0\\ \amp = \left( 0-\frac{e^{0}}{2}\right)-\left( -\frac{(-{2})^3}{3}-\frac{e^{{4}}}{2}\right) \\ \amp = \frac{1}{6}(3 e^{4} - {19})\approx {24.1324} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
