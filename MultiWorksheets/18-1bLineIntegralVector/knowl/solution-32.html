Solution 6.6.1.

Remember that if \(\vec{r}(t)\text{,}\) for \(a\leq t\leq b\text{,}\) is a smooth parameterization of an oriented curve \(C\) and \(\vec{F}\) is a vector field which is continuous on \(C\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} \int_C \vec{F}\cdot d\vec{r}=\int_a^b \vec{F}(\vec{r}(t))\cdot \vec{r}'(t) dt. \end{equation*}

The line segment from \(({4},{7})\) to \(({0},{7})\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(t)=(-{4}t+{4})\vec{i} +{7}\vec{j},\quad 0\leq t\leq 1 \end{equation*}

and we have

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}'(t)=-{4}\vec{i} . \end{equation*}

Thus,

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int_{C} \left( x\vec{i} -y\vec{j} \right) \cdot d\vec{r} \amp = \int_0^1 \left( (-{4}t+{4})\vec{i}-{7}\vec{j}\right) \cdot \left(-{4}\vec{i} \right)dt\\ \amp = \int_0^1 {16}t-{16} \,dt \\ \amp = \left( {8}t^2-{16}t\right)\big|_{0}^{1} \\ \amp = {-8}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
