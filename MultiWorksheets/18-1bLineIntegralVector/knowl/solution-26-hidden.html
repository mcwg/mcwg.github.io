Remember that if \(\vec{r}(t)\text{,}\) for \(a\leq t\leq b\text{,}\) is a smooth parameterization of an oriented curve \(C\) and \(\vec{F}\) is a vector field which is continuous on \(C\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} \int_C \vec{F}\cdot d\vec{r}=\int_a^b \vec{F}(\vec{r}(t))\cdot \vec{r}\,'(t) dt. \end{equation*}

The line segment from \(({7},{8})\) to \(({-1},{8})\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(t)=(-{8}t+{7})\vec{i} +{8}\vec{j},\quad 0\leq t\leq 1 \end{equation*}

and we have

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}\,'(t)=-{8}\vec{i} . \end{equation*}

Thus,

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \int_{C} \left( x\vec{i} -y\vec{j} \right) \cdot d\vec{r} \amp = \int_0^1 \left( (-{8}t+{7})\vec{i}-{8}\vec{j}\right) \cdot \left(-{8}\vec{i} \right)dt\\ \amp = \int_0^1 {64}t-{56} \,dt \\ \amp = \left( {32}t^2-{56}t\right)\big|_{0}^{1} \\ \amp = {-24}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
