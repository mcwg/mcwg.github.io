Solution 6.2.1.

Remember that if \(\vec{r}(t)\text{,}\) for \(a\leq t\leq b\text{,}\) is a smooth parameterization of an oriented curve \(C\) and \(\vec{F}\) is a vector field which is continuous on \(C\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} \int_C \vec{F}\cdot d\vec{r}=\int_a^b \vec{F}(\vec{r}(t))\cdot \vec{r}\,'(t) dt. \end{equation*}

The line segment from \(({8},0,{2})\) to \(({8},{5},{4})\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(t)={8}\vec{i} +{5}t\vec{j}+({2}t+{2})\vec{k},\quad 0\leq t\leq 1 \end{equation*}

and we have

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}\,'(t)={5}\vec{j} +{2}\vec{k}. \end{equation*}

Thus,

\begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} \int_{C} \left( z\vec{i} -y\vec{j}+x\vec{k} \right) \cdot d\vec{r} \amp = \int_0^1 \left( ({2}t+{2})\vec{i}-{5}t\vec{j}+{8}\vec{k}\right) \cdot \left({5}\vec{j}+{2}\vec{k} \right)dt\\ \amp = \int_0^1 (-{25}t+{16}) dt \\ \amp = \left( -{{\textstyle\frac{25}{2}}}t^2+{16}t\right)\big|_{0}^{1}\\ \amp = {{\textstyle\frac{7}{2}}}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
