Parametrize the curve and remember that if \(\vec{r}(t)\text{,}\) for \(a\leq t\leq b\text{,}\) is a smooth parameterization of an oriented curve \(C\) and \(\vec{F}\) is a vector field which is continuous on \(C\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} \int_C \vec{F}\cdot d\vec{r}=\int_a^b \vec{F}(\vec{r}(t))\cdot \vec{r}\,'(t) dt. \end{equation*}
