Remember that if \(\vec{r}(t)\text{,}\) for \(a\leq t\leq b\text{,}\) is a smooth parameterization of an oriented curve \(C\) and \(\vec{F}\) is a vector field which is continuous on \(C\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} \int_C \vec{F}\cdot d\vec{r}=\int_a^b \vec{F}(\vec{r}(t))\cdot \vec{r}'(t) dt. \end{equation*}

The part of the circle of radius \({6}\) centered at the origin in the first quadrant from \(({6},0)\) to \((0,{6})\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(t)={6}\cos(t)\vec{i}+{6}\sin(t)\vec{j},\quad 0\leq t\leq \pi/2, \end{equation*}

and so we have

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}'(t)=-{6}\sin(t)\vec{i}+{6}\cos(t)\vec{j}. \end{equation*}

Thus,

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int_{C} \left( \vec{i} -y\vec{j} \right) \cdot d\vec{r} \amp = \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \left( \vec{i}-{6}\sin(t)\vec{j}\right) \cdot \left(-{6}\sin(t)\vec{i}+{6}\cos(t)\vec{j} \right)dt\\ \amp = \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \left(-{6}\sin(t)-{36}\sin(t)\cos(t) \right) dt \\ \amp = \left( {6}\cos{(t)} -{18}\sin^2{(t)}\right)\big|_{0}^{\pi /2} \\ \amp = \left( 0 -{18} \right)-\left( {6} -0\right) \\ \amp = {-24} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
