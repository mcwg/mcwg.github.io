Remember that if \(\vec{r}(t)\text{,}\) for \(a\leq t\leq b\text{,}\) is a smooth parameterization of an oriented curve \(C\) and \(\vec{F}\) is a vector field which is continuous on \(C\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} \int_C \vec{F}\cdot d\vec{r}=\int_a^b \vec{F}(\vec{r}(t))\cdot \vec{r}'(t) dt. \end{equation*}

The part of the circle of radius \(\) centered at the origin in the first quadrant from \((,0)\) to \((0,)\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(t)=\cos(t)\vec{i}+\sin(t)\vec{j},\quad 0\leq t\leq \pi/2, \end{equation*}

and so we have

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}'(t)=-\sin(t)\vec{i}+\cos(t)\vec{j}. \end{equation*}
