Solution 6.5.1.

Remember that if \(\vec{r}(t)\text{,}\) for \(a\leq t\leq b\text{,}\) is a smooth parameterization of an oriented curve \(C\) and \(\vec{F}\) is a vector field which is continuous on \(C\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} \int_C \vec{F}\cdot d\vec{r}=\int_a^b \vec{F}(\vec{r}(t))\cdot \vec{r}'(t) dt. \end{equation*}

The curve \(x=\cos{(y)}\) from \((1,0)\) to \((1,\pi)\) can be parameterized by \(\cos(t)\vec{i}+t\vec{j}\) with \(0\leq t\leq \pi\text{.}\) To find a parameterization from \((1,\pi)\) to \((1,0)\) we reverse the orientation by replacing \(t\) by \(-t\text{.}\) Thus, the curve \(x=\cos{(y)}\) from \((1,\pi)\) to \((1,0)\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(t)=\cos(t)\vec{i}-t\vec{j},\quad -\pi\leq t\leq 0, \end{equation*}

(since \(\cos(-t)=\cos(t)\)), and we have

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}'(t)=-\sin(t)\vec{i}-\vec{j}. \end{equation*}

Thus,

\begin{align*} \int_{C} \left(y\vec{i} +2x\vec{j} \right) \cdot d\vec{r} \amp = \int_{-\pi}^{0} \left( -t\vec{i}+2\cos(t)\vec{j}\right) \cdot \left(-\sin(t)\vec{i}-\vec{j} \right)dt\\ \amp = \int_{-\pi}^{0} \left( t\sin(t)-2\cos(t) \right) dt\\ \amp = \left(\sin{(t)} -t\cos{(t)} -2\sin(t)\right)\big|_{-\pi }^{0} \\ \amp = 0-\left(0 -(-\pi)-0\right) \\ \amp = -\pi. \end{align*}
