Item.
By the way it is defined, the line integral \(\int_{C} \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r}\) is a scalar measurement of the flow of the vector field \(\vec{F}\) along the curve \(C\text{:}\) that is, the integral is positive if \(\vec{F}\) generally flows in the same direction as \(C\text{,}\) negative if \(\vec{F}\) generally flows in the opposite direction to \(C\text{,}\) and zero if perpendicular or there is the same flow with as against.
