The curve \(x=\cos{(y)}\) from \((1,0)\) to \((1,\pi)\) can be parameterized by \(\cos(t)\vec{i}+t\vec{j}\) with \(0\leq t\leq \pi\text{.}\) To find a parameterization from \((1,\pi)\) to \((1,0)\) we reverse the orientation by replacing \(t\) by \(-t\text{.}\) Thus, the curve \(x=\cos{(y)}\) from \((1,\pi)\) to \((1,0)\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(t)=\cos(t)\vec{i}-t\vec{j},\quad -\pi\leq t\leq 0, \end{equation*}

(since \(\cos(-t)=\cos(t)\)), and we have

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}'(t)=-\sin(t)\vec{i}-\vec{j}. \end{equation*}
