The curve \(y=e^x\) from \((0,1)\) to \((,e^)\) can be parameterized by \(t\vec{i}+(e^{t})\vec{j}\) with \(0\leq t\leq \text{.}\) To find a parameterization from \((,e^)\) to \((0,1)\) we reverse the orientation by replacing \(t\) by \(-t\text{.}\) Thus, the curve \(y=e^x\) from \((,e^)\) to \((0,1)\) can be parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}(t)=-t\vec{i}+(e^{-t})\vec{j},\quad -\leq t\leq 0, \end{equation*}

and we have

\begin{equation*} \vec{r}'(t)=-\vec{i} -e^{-t}\vec{j}. \end{equation*}
