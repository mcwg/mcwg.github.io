1. The vector \(\vec{PQ}\) has its tail at \(P\) and head to \(Q\text{.}\) The components are the difference in coordinates of the points \(P\) and \(Q\text{,}\) so \(\vec{PQ}=\langle -1-(-{2}),{1}-1,{4}-{3}\rangle=\langle{1},{0},{1}\rangle \text{.}\) Thus

    \begin{equation*} ||\vec{PQ}||=\sqrt{({1})^2+({0})^2+({1})^2} =\sqrt{{2}}. \end{equation*}

  2. If \(\vec{v}+\vec{w}\) has its tail placed at \(P\text{,}\) the coordinates of the point at its head will be the sum of the coordinates of \(Q\) with the components of \(\vec{v}+\vec{w}\text{.}\) We have \(\vec{v}+\vec{w}=\langle {-5},{8},{5}\rangle\) and so the point is \((-{2}+({-5}),1+{8},{3}+{5})=({-7},{9},{8})\text{.}\)

  3. We calculate using the definition of scalar multiplication and addition. We have

    \begin{equation*} \newcommand{\amp}{\amp }\begin{aligned} {4}\vec{w}-{2}\vec{v} \amp= {4}\cdot \langle -{4},{3},0\rangle-{2}\cdot \langle -1,{5},{5}\rangle \\ \amp= \langle -{16},{12},0\rangle +\langle {2},-{10},-{10}\rangle \\ \amp= \langle {-14},{2},-{10}\rangle \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

  4. We have \({9}\vec{PQ} ={9}\cdot \langle {1},{0},{1}\rangle =\langle{9},{0},{9}\rangle\text{,}\) so

    \begin{equation*} ||{9}\vec{PQ}||=\sqrt{({9})^2+({0})^2+({9})^2} =\sqrt{{162}}. \end{equation*}

  5. Since \(\vec{PQ}\) and \(\vec{QP}\) are identical vectors but pointing in opposite directions, \(\vec{PQ} +\vec{QP}=\vec{0}\text{.}\)
