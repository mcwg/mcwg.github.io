Solution 4.1.1.

  1. \(\vec{PQ}\) is the vector with its tail at \(P\) and head to \(Q\text{.}\) The components are the difference in coordinates of the points \(P\) and \(Q\text{,}\) so \(\vec{PQ}=\langle{5}-{1},{5}-{4},{2}-{1}\rangle=\langle{4},{1},{1}\rangle\text{.}\)

  2. \(\vec{QP}\) is the vector with its tail at \(Q\) and head to \(P\text{.}\) The components are the difference in coordinates of the points \(Q\) and \(P\text{,}\) so \(\vec{QP}=\langle{1}-{5},{4}-{5},{1}-{2}\rangle=\langle{-4},{-1},{-1}\rangle\text{.}\)

  3. If \(\vec{v}\) has its tail placed at \(Q\text{,}\) the coordinates of the point at its head will be the sum of the coordinates of \(Q\) with the components of \(\vec{v}\text{,}\) so the point has coordinates \(({5}+(-{5}),{5}+1,{2}+0)=({0},{6},{2})\text{.}\)
