Solution 4.6.1.

First, the magnitude of \(\vec{v} ={8\mathit{\vec i}+4\mathit{\vec j}+8\mathit{\vec k}}\) is \(||\vec{v}|| =\sqrt{({8})^2+({4})^2+({8})^2} =\sqrt{{144}}={12}\text{.}\) Then a unit vector pointing in the same direction as \(\vec{v}\) is

\begin{equation*} \frac{\vec{v}}{||\vec{v}||} ={{\textstyle\frac{1}{12}}} \left( {8\mathit{\vec i}+4\mathit{\vec j}+8\mathit{\vec k}}\right) = {\frac{2}{3}\mathit{\vec i}+\frac{1}{3}\mathit{\vec j}+\frac{2}{3}\mathit{\vec k}}. \end{equation*}

To obtain a vector of length \({4}\) from a unit vector and in opposite direction, we can scale by a factor of \(-{4}\text{,}\) giving

\begin{equation*} -{4}\cdot \left( {\frac{2}{3}\mathit{\vec i}+\frac{1}{3}\mathit{\vec j}+\frac{2}{3}\mathit{\vec k}} \right) = {\frac{-8}{3}\mathit{\vec i}+\frac{-4}{3}\mathit{\vec j}+\frac{-8}{3}\mathit{\vec k}}. \end{equation*}
