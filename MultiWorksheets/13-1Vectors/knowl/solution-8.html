Solution 4.7.1.

First, the magnitude of \(\vec{v} ={2\mathit{\vec i}+4\mathit{\vec j}-4\mathit{\vec k}}\) is \(||\vec{v}|| =\sqrt{({2})^2+({4})^2+({-4})^2} =\sqrt{{36}}={6}\text{.}\) Then a unit vector pointing in the same direction as \(\vec{v}\) is

\begin{equation*} \frac{\vec{v}}{||\vec{v}||} ={{\textstyle\frac{1}{6}}} \left( {2\mathit{\vec i}+4\mathit{\vec j}-4\mathit{\vec k}}\right) = {\frac{1}{3}\mathit{\vec i}+\frac{2}{3}\mathit{\vec j}+\frac{-2}{3}\mathit{\vec k}}. \end{equation*}

To obtain a vector of length \({5}\) from a unit vector and in opposite direction, we can scale by a factor of \(-{5}\text{,}\) giving

\begin{equation*} -{5}\cdot \left( {\frac{1}{3}\mathit{\vec i}+\frac{2}{3}\mathit{\vec j}+\frac{-2}{3}\mathit{\vec k}} \right) = {\frac{-5}{3}\mathit{\vec i}+\frac{-10}{3}\mathit{\vec j}+\frac{10}{3}\mathit{\vec k}}. \end{equation*}
