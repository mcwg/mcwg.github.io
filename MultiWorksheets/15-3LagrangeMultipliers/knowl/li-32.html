Item 5.2.c.

We know that \(f(x,y)\) has a global maximum and minimum subject to the constraint because the constraint is closed and bounded. A global minimum or maximum of \(f\) subject to the constraint \(x^2+y^2\leq 1\) will either be a minimum or maximum on the boundary \(x^2+y^2=1\) and can therefore be found using Lagrange multipliers, or will occur at a critical point on the inside \(\newcommand{\lt}{\lt }x^2+y^2\lt 1\text{.}\) We already found the global minimum and maximum on the boundary in (a), so we just need to compare to the value at the critical points.

From (b), the only critical point is \((0,0)\text{,}\) which does occur inside the boundary. However, since \(f(0,0)={6}\) is not bigger than the global maximum on the boundary and not smaller than the global minimum on the boundary, the maximum and minimum values subject to the constraint \(x^2+y^2\le 1\) are the same as the ones subject to the constaint \(x^2+y^2=1\text{.}\)
