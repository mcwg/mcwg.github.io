If \(\lambda =1\text{,}\) then the equation \(({4}-2y)=\lambda (2y)\) becomes \(({4}-2y)=2y\) or \(4y={4}\text{,}\) so \(y={1}\text{.}\) Plugging into the equation \(x^2+y^2={16}\text{,}\) this gives \(x^2={15}\) or \(x=\pm \sqrt{{15}}\text{.}\) This gives the points \((\sqrt{{15}},{1})\) and \((-\sqrt{{15}},{1})\text{.}\)
