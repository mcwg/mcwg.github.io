Solution 5.3.1.

We use Lagrange multipliers with constraint functions \(g(x,y,z)=x^2+y^2\) and \(h(x,y,z)=x+y+z\text{.}\) Note that the restriction \(g(x,y,z)={25}\) defines a cylinder centered along the \(z\)-axis and the restriction \(h(x,y,z)={5}\) defines a plane. The restrictions \(g(x,y,z)={25}\) and \(h(x,y,z)={5}\) together define the intersection of these two surfaces, which will be an ellipse. Thus, the restriction is closed and bounded, and so we know that \(f(x,y,z)\) will have a global maximum and minimum subject to these restrictions which we can find with the method of Lagrange Multipliers.

We have \(\nabla f=2x\vec{i} +2y\vec{j} -2z\vec{k}\text{,}\) \(\nabla g=2x\vec{i} +2y\vec{j}\text{,}\) \(\nabla h =\vec{i}+\vec{j} +\vec{k}\) and we are trying to solve \(\nabla f=\lambda \nabla g +\mu \nabla h\text{.}\) Resolving components, this means we are trying to solve the equations

\begin{equation*} 2x=\lambda 2x +\mu, \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 2y=\lambda 2y +\mu, \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} -2z=0 +\mu \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} x^2+y^2={25} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} x+y+z={5} \end{equation*}

Subtracting the first two equations we find \(2(x-y)=\lambda\cdot 2(x-y)\text{,}\) and so we must have \(\lambda =1\) or \(x=y\text{.}\)

If \(\lambda=1\text{,}\) then the first equation tells us that \(2x=2x+\mu\text{,}\) so \(\mu=0\text{.}\) Then, the fact that \(-2z=\mu\) gives \(z=0\text{.}\) Thus, by the last equation we know that \(x+y={5}\text{,}\) which together with \(x^2+y^2={25}\) implies that either \(x=0\) and \(y={5}\) or \(y=0\) and \(x={5}\text{.}\) Thus, the solutions to the system of equations when \(\lambda=1\) are \(({5},0,0)\text{,}\) \((0,{5},0)\text{.}\)

If instead of \(\lambda=1\) we have \(x = y\text{,}\) then \(x^2+y^2={25}\) implies that \(2x^2={25}\text{,}\) so \(x=y=\pm{5}/\sqrt{2}\text{.}\) Using \(x+y+z={5}\) to find the value of \(z\text{,}\) we find that the solutions to the system when \(x=y\) are \(\left({5}/\sqrt{2},{5}/\sqrt{2},{5}-{5}\sqrt{2}\right)\) and \(\left(-{5}/\sqrt{2},-{5}/\sqrt{2},{5}+{5}\sqrt{2}\right)\text{.}\)

Plugging all these points into \(f(x,y,z)\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} f({5},0,0) ={25}, \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f(0,{5},0) ={25} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f\left({5}/\sqrt{2},{5}/\sqrt{2},{5}-{5}\sqrt{2}\right) ={50\sqrt{2}-50} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f\left(-{5}/\sqrt{2},-{5}/\sqrt{2},{5}+{5}\sqrt{2}\right) = {-50-50\sqrt{2}} \end{equation*}

Thus, the minimum value of \(f\) subject to the restriction is \({-50-50\sqrt{2}}\text{,}\) and the maximum is \({25}\text{.}\)
