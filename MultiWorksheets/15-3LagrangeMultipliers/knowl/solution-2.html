Solution 4.2.1.

Sketching the line \({y}={1}\) on the contour diagram, we see that it is tangent to the \({1}\)-contour at the point \({\left(0,1\right)}\text{,}\) and that \(f(x,y)\) increases in both directions along this line from this point, see the figure. It follows that the global minimum is \({1}\) at the point \({\left(0,1\right)}\) and \(f(x,y)\) has no global maximum.
