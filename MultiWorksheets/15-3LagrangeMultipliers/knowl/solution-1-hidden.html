We know that \(f(x,y)\) has a global maximum and minimum subject to the constraint because the constraint is closed and bounded. Sketching the circle \(x^2+y^2={1}\) on the contour diagram, we see that it is tangent to the \({-2}\)-contour at the points \({\left(0,1\right)}\) and \({\left(0,-1\right)}\text{,}\) and tangent to the \({1}\)-contour at the points \({\left(1,0\right)}\) and \({\left(-1,0\right)}\text{.}\) There are no other points where it is tangent, so these are the only places a global minimum or maximum can occur subject to this constraint. It follows that \({-2}\) is the global minimum and \({1}\) is the global maximum.
