Solution 5.6.4.1.
  1. Varying \(A\) does not change the maximum value, but increasing \(A\) moves the time when the maximum is achieved to the right.
  2. Varying \(k\) changes both the maximum value, and the time when the maximum is achieved. The larger \(k\) is the earlier the maximum is achieved and the narrower the graph. The smaller it is the later the maximum is achieved and the smaller the maximum value.
  3. Varying \(L\) doesn't change the time when the maximum is achieved but the larger the \(L\) value, the larger the value of the maximum value of \(N\text{.}\)
