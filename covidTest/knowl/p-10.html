Using the method of separation of variables and partial fraction expansions we can show that the solution to

\begin{equation*} dP/dt=kP(L-P)\qquad P(0)=P_0 \end{equation*}

is given by

\begin{equation*} P(t)=\frac{\left(\frac{P_0}{L-P_0}\right)Le^{kt}}{1+\left(\frac{P_0}{L-P_0}\right)e^{kt}}. \end{equation*}

We can simplify this expression by setting \(A=\frac{P_0}{L-P_0}\) and we have

\begin{equation*} P(t)=\frac{ALe^{kt}}{1+Ae^{kt}}\qquad\text{and}\qquad dP/dt=\frac{LAke^{kt}}{\left(e^{kt}+A\right)^2}. \end{equation*}
